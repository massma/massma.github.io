.SHORT TERM /TONIGHT THROUGH THURSDAY NIGHT/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Widespread stratus
this afternoon with onshore flow as west- northwest flow aloft
continues due to troughing located across Western US. Generally

.AVIATION...
-- Changed Discussion --
Satellite shows some of cloud cover beginning to
lift and scatter behind a trough that past through earlier
(especially towards the north). Overcast ceilings will become more
broken this afternoon, and most areas (except along the Pacific
Coast) will see MVFR ceilings improve to VFR around or just after
21Z (from north to south). The flow aloft will turn more

front tonight/tomorrow morning with scattered showers across the

Winds this afternoon remain out of the southwest 5 to 10 kt (few
gusts this afternoon near the waters, and variable northerly winds
north of Seattle with diffluence with a push from the Strait of Juan
de Fuca). Winds will pick up in the South Interior from the west
on early Wednesday.

.MARINE...
-- Changed Discussion --
Higher pressure in the outer waters (with lower pressure
inland) will continue the pattern of onshore flow (with westerly
pushes through at least Wednesday evening). Seas this afternoon
continue to remain 8-10 feet in the coastal with periods around 9
seconds with the small craft advisory in place. Gusty winds
continue in the outer coastal waters with winds of 15 to 25 kt
(will decrease going into Wednesday). A push through the Strait
of Juan de Fuca will bring 20-30 kt westerlies through (another
round is expected behind a cold front Wednesday afternoon into
Thursday).

The next chance of hazardous gusty winds will come with a system
Sunday in the outer coastal waters. Otherwise seas will drop to 5
to 6 feet Thursday, and increase to 6 to 8 feet Friday through the
weekend then dropping back to 5 to 6 feet early next week.
