18

From Previous Discussion...Overall little change in the forecast
pattern: A large upper level ridge centered over the Four Corners
continues to be the driving mechanism for this week`s weather.
Marine stratus has largely burned off except for along the immediate
coast, with temperatures across the region ranging from the mid 60s
to the low 80s as of X. An upper level trough sitting just offshore
will move across western Washington tomorrow night into Tuesday.
Monday will be a touch cooler than today, with highs in the mid 70s
to low 80s. Onshore flow will increase as the trough moves through
on Tuesday, allowing temperatures to dip down closer to normal, in
the upper 70s to around 80. As the trough departs on Wednesday,
temperatures will warm back up into mid 70s to mid 80s as before.
The marine stratus layer will continue to be a feature across the
area each morning, with low temperatures in the 50s to low 60s.

.LONG TERM /THURSDAY THROUGH SUNDAY/...From Previous
Discussion...Deterministic and ensemble guidance indicates continued
above average temperatures, with highs rising into the upper 80s to
low 90s by the end of the week and into next weekend. Flow aloft
becomes zonal as a trough retrogrades through the Canadian Prairies.
Another trough then develops Friday into the weekend over Alberta/BC
and extends down into the region. These features will promote the
potential for elevated convection each afternoon over the Cascades
(in particular the North Cascades towards the Canadian border), with
up to a 15-25% chance for showers and thunderstorms.

From Previous Discussion...A rather persistent pattern
will continue across the region through early next week with a broad
