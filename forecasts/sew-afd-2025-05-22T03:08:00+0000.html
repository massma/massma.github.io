.SYNOPSIS...Increasing clouds tonight as a weak disturbance moves
across the region, with light rain possible south of Puget Sound and
in the mountains. A drier and warmer pattern develops late this week
and especially into the weekend with high pressure building overhead.
Confidence is lower for the start of next week, with the potential

.SHORT TERM /TONIGHT THROUGH SATURDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
While the increased
cloud cover will be the most impactful change to sensible
weather, shower chances will also increase for areas south of
Puget Sound and extending into the central Cascades late
tonight and into early Thursday. This disturbance will shift
eastward rather quickly, with much of the area dry by Thursday
evening (though showers likely linger in the Cascades Thursday
night). Otherwise, a shift in the pattern continues to appear
likely by Friday as high pressure begins to build into the
region. Mostly dry conditions Friday with temperatures returning
to near-normal values.

.LONG TERM /SUNDAY THROUGH WEDNESDAY/...High pressure will
then continue to amplify over the Pacific Northwest this
weekend, bringing a steadier warmer and drier pattern.
Temperatures on Sunday are expected to climb well into the 70s
with the warmest spots even making a run at the 80 degree mark.
This is likely to be the warmest day of the stretch, with the
potential for the ridge to weaken early next week. The ensemble
guidance does begin to diverge next week, however, as the
solutions become split between a stubborn ridge and splitting
systems keeping the area mostly dry or the ridge weakening with
the next trough crossing the region. For now, rain chances
return by midweek with temperatures remaining near or slightly
above seasonal norms.
-- End Changed Discussion --

.MARINE...High pressure over the coastal waters will weaken slightly
today as a surface low moves towards Oregon and Northern California.
Northwesterly flow across the coastal waters will increase this
evening to around 10-15 kt and gradually subside into Thursday
morning. High pressure rebuilds over the waters on Thursday and
persists into the weekend. Model guidance shows a low pressure
system may try to move over the coastal waters on Friday; however,
at this time it looks like it will dissipate before it reaches the
coastal waters. Diurnal westerly pushes across the Strait of Juan de
Fuca will also occur over the next few days (starting on Thursday).
At this time, sustained winds and gusts look to remain below small
craft criteria. The next frontal system will move over the coastal
waters on Monday.

Seas hovering around 6-7 ft this afternoon will continue to subside
into Thursday and Friday to around 3-5 ft. Going into the weekend,
seas will rise slightly to around 5-7 ft and persist into early next
week.
