Zonal flow aloft and increased onshore flow this morning is
bringing in a stronger marine push, with low stratus overspreading
much of western Washington. While ceilings will improve
throughout the late morning and into the afternoon, skies will
stay cloudy through the rest of the day. Temperatures today will
peak in the mid 60s to mid 70s, which will be the warmest day for
the remainder of the week.

Temperatures Tuesday will fall a few degrees as a trough deepens
along western Canada. Onshore flow will continue, with another
morning marine push and cloudy skies throughout the day with the
lowlands seeing highs near the 70 degree mark.

Forecast models have slowed down the progression of the next
incoming system, with the next chance of showers spreading inland
late Tuesday evening into Wednesday. A cutoff low will swiftly
track inland across the Pacific Northwest throughout the day on
Wednesday, sliding a cold front across the region that will lower
temperatures into the 60s. Ensembles have increased rainfall
amounts with this system, with roughly a quarter of an inch for
most lowland areas and upwards of half an inch across Grays Harbor
County and over the mountains. Mount Rainier will also see a few
inches of snow accumulation over its highest peaks as more fall-
like conditions return to the region.

.LONG TERM /THURSDAY THROUGH SUNDAY/...Light shower activity will
linger over the Cascades and foothills early Thursday as the
trough exits to the east. Conditions will briefly dry out
throughout the day Thursday ahead of the next incoming trough
that will pass over the region throughout the weekend. Periods of
light shower activity will continue through Sunday, with below-
normal temperatures and cloudy skies persisting.
