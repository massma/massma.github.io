.SHORT TERM /TONIGHT THROUGH SATURDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Weak high pressure will
continue to build throughout the region, with clouds gradually
scattering out for a pleasant evening. Temperatures will warm
into the mid 70s for the interior and mid 60s for the coastal
locations.

The aforementioned high pressure will get replaced by weak
troughing over the Pacific Northwest starting on Thursday, with
more cloud cover, cooler temps, and mountain showers. Along with
mountain showers, there is a chance of thunderstorms primarily in
the North Cascades Thursday afternoon and evening. High temps
will generally be in the mid 70s.

An upper level low centered in Canada will gradually drop down and
center just offshore our area on Friday, with a continued chance
of showers and thunderstorms primarily in the North Cascades. This
upper level low will continue to spin offshore on Saturday,
placing western Washington in a moist S/SW flow.

Latest guidance suggests widespread shower coverage for most of
the area on Saturday. Along with increased precipitation chances
that we have not seen in a while, there is a threat of
thunderstorms for most of the lowlands and Cascades. Probabilistic
guidance suggests around a 15-25% of thunderstorms late Saturday
afternoon into the evening hours, increasing to around 30% through
the overnight hours into early Sunday morning. There is
definitely some ensemble uncertainty regarding the position of
the upper level low offshore, which could lead to differences in
rain amounts, timings, and thunderstorm chances. As for now, we
will continue to monitor the threat as new guidance becomes
available.

.LONG TERM /SUNDAY THROUGH WEDNESDAY/...Ensemble guidance
continues to highlight S/SW flow for a chance of showers and
thunderstorms through Sunday. Conditions look to trend drier
heading into Tuesday, with ensembles struggling on precipitation
chances later on in the week.

Mazurkiewicz
