A high-amplitude ridge will
exit the region tonight while a weak frontal system moves inland.

.AVIATION...
-- Changed Discussion --
A positively tilted upper level ridge over Western
Washington this afternoon is giving way to a weak shortwave trough,
driving a weak frontal system towards the north. High clouds have
begun to build in ahead of this system, and this has burned off
nearly all of the low stratus/fog from this morning (although
pockets remain in the Cascades and also around the Olympics/San
Juans/Kitsap Peninsula. Remaining low stratus will likely clear by
00Z. Winds this afternoon have been light (and sometimes variable)
at or under 3-5 kt.

The previously mentioned frontal system tonight will produce
isolated to scattered showers over Western Washington from west to
east, from 00Z-12Z. Once showers dissipate, ceilings will drop down
to significantly, with widespread IFR/LIFR expected (especially for
southern terminals). Visibilities may also be as low as a quarter of
a mile in spots (if fog develops). Terminals least likely to drop
that far down (remaining at MVFR) are terminals along the Pacific
Coast/Strait of Juan de Fuca). This round of stratus is expected to
be shorter in duration (13Z-21Z) as high pressure builds back in and
clears most of the cloud coverage by Thursday afternoon. Winds will
be light and variable under 3 kt (but will have more of a northerly
component towards Wednesday afternoon/evening).

KSEA...High clouds over the terminal this afternoon (though a few
low clouds around 600 ft remain but will clear this afternoon).
Isolated showers will be in and near the terminal between 06Z-12Z
Wednesday morning. Probabilities for IFR are at 30% (and 20% for
LIFR). Best time for low ceilings/mist or fog to develop is from 15Z-
20Z, with clearing to VFR expected to take place quickly after.
Light winds under 5 kt are expected (will favor a southerly
direction through Wednesday afternoon before turning more
northeast). Variable directions are possible at times.

HPR

.MARINE...
-- Changed Discussion --
A positively tilted upper level ridge will move inland
with a weak shortwave trough expected to swing a frontal system over
the waters tonight. Isolated showers are expected Tuesday afternoon
into Wednesday morning. Winds have decreased over the coastal waters
(and the current small craft advisory in place is for high seas of 8
to 12 feet through Wednesday morning). Once the front passes
through, high pressure will build back over land (resulting in light
offshore winds with an additional system expected to arrive Friday).
There will be the potential for morning fog Wednesday morning in
some of the inland waters (and at times next weekend in between
storm systems). Otherwise, the next chance of gusty winds will come
Friday with the next weather maker. These winds are only expected to
be hazardous to small craft. The seas Wednesday into Thursday will
drop 6 to 8 feet, picking back up to 10 to 15 feet Friday into
Saturday before dropping back under 10 feet next weekend.

HPR
