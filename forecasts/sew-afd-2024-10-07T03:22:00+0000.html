.SHORT TERM /TONIGHT THROUGH WEDNESDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
A rather pleasant fall
day in western Washington today with temperatures in the upper
50s to low 60s and mostly clear skies. A few high clouds will pass
overhead. Clear conditions continue tonight with fog and low
stratus reforming along the coast and in the low spots and river
valleys through the interior, similar to what occurred this
morning. Lows should be a touch warmer, in the low to mid 40s.

A weak upper level trough currently overhead will move off to the
east into Monday, allowing for a trough to move southeastward from
British Columbia closer to the region. This will allow for chances
of rain to begin in the northwestern-most portions of the area
Monday afternoon into Monday evening. Most of western Washington
will remain partly cloudy throughout the day, allowing for Monday
to be the warmest day of the week, with highs reaching the upper
60s to mid 70s.

The front will continue to slowly drift southward on Tuesday and
weaken as it does so. Chances for rain showers are expected on
Tuesday, but amounts will be light. Overall, less than a tenth of
an inch of rain is expected, with the highest amounts (up to a
quarter to a half of an inch of rain) over the northwestern part
of the Olympic Peninsula.

The pattern remains with a broad trough over the eastern Pacific
with several waves moving through it. Chances for light rain will
continue into Wednesday but not amount to much; chances taper
throughout the day.

.LONG TERM /THURSDAY THROUGH SUNDAY/...A weak ridge builds over
the area on Thursday, providing a break in the precipitation
chances. Following, the patter evolution becomes more uncertain
with larger spread amongst model ensemble members. The general
pattern favors a longwave trough over the eastern Pacific with a
ridge over the intermountain west. Depending on the placement and
amplitude of these features, this could allow systems to work
their way into western Washington, bringing additional rain
chances. Many members, however, remain dry through the weekend.
The mean solution also favors a warming trend, with temperatures
warming back up into the mid to upper 60s, even perhaps the low
70s again in some spots.
