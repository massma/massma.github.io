.SHORT TERM /TODAY THROUGH MONDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
A weak front will pass
through western WA early this morning with light rainfall over the
region. Most areas will just see a trace to a few hundredths.
We`re under post-frontal onshore flow through the remainder of the
day leading to cooler conditions and highs in the 50s to around
60.

Onshore flow will persist into Sunday and showers will increase
with a passing trough axis. Most of the showers will be along the
coast and mountains, with the focus shifting to the central sound

Monday will be drier but also cooler under NW flow. Forecast
highs are in the 50s with lows Monday night in the 30s to lower
40s. Areas around the south sound may see freezing temps with lows
in the lower 30s possible. 33

.AVIATION...
-- Changed Discussion --
A quick trough this morning will swing a cold front
through the region. The flow aloft will weaken from southwesterly to
westerly through Sunday as the trough broadens out over western WA,
before picking back up from the northwest as high pressure builds
in.

Radar was showing a line of rain showers moving across all
terminals this morning, reaching Puget Sound just shy of 09Z. The
showers have been light (not reaching the ground in some spots), but
ceilings have begun to drop to MVFR along the coast under the front
(it remains comfortably VFR this morning elsewhere). Hi-res guidance
has the line breaking up as it approaches the Cascades just before
sunrise (~12Z). A few of the model members keep a convergence zone
going through late this morning in and around Puget Sound. Few lower
ceilings down to MVFR are possible with this, as are possible wind
shifts and an isolated gust to 20 kt. Any convergence zone activity
is expected to wrap up ~18Z, with some partial clearing taking place
in the South Sound later today.

Ceilings today will remain VFR in most places, with widespread drops
down to MVFR expected going into Sunday morning with a strong
onshore push. Couple breezy winds to 20 kt are possible along the
Pacific Coast/Strait of Juan de Fuca areas later today. Otherwise
expect west/northwest winds 5 to 10 kt this afternoon, with wind
shifting to the southwest expected later tonight/Sunday behind the
front.

KSEA...Cold front is bringing showers through early this morning.
Some guidance is hinting at a lingering convergence zone continuing
shower activity up to 18Z. This will likely drop ceilings down to
MVFR from roughly 12Z to 18Z before improving to VFR. Winds may get
a little squarely with the convergence zone, but direction is
expected to be out of the north today are around 5 to 10 kt. A shift
to the south is expected around 05Z late Saturday evening. Lower
ceilings down to MVFR are likely Sunday morning.

HPR

.MARINE...
-- Changed Discussion --
A quick moving cold front will sweep a line of showers
over the waters early this morning. The trough will elongate a bit
going into Sunday before building high pressure takes over the
trough going into Monday. Gusty winds will continue this weekend,
prompting another round of small craft advisories for the entire
Strait of Juan de Fuca and Admiralty Inlet (especially as high
pressure builds in post-front). Another push is expected Sunday
night into Monday with breezy winds through the Strait of Juan de
Fuca.

Coastal waters: besides the wind decreasing today, seas will
continue to remain hazardous for the small craft (primarily due to
seas of 8 to 10 ft at 8 seconds continuing through partway into
Sunday). Additional breezy winds are possible in the northern marine
zones Sunday into Monday with the next system. A drop in seas down
to 5 to 7 feet is expected on Tuesday.

HPR
