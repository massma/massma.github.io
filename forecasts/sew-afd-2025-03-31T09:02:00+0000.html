.SHORT TERM /TODAY THROUGH WEDNESDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Scattered showers continue
to rotate onshore across Western Washington this morning around a
broad trough of low pressure offshore. We`ll see something of a
lull in shower activity later this morning before the next upper
disturbance rotates northward across the area for an uptick in
activity this afternoon and evening. High temperatures today will
cool to slightly below normal. The low pressure center will move
onshore over Oregon early Tuesday. Showers chances will linger
across Western Washington, but most areas will be dry much of the
day with the best probabilities for precip in the mountains. An
increase in onshore flow could lead to a brief convergence zone
Tuesday afternoon or early evening. Broad upper troughing remains
over Western Washington into Wednesday for a continued chance of
showers and temperatures remaining a little below normal.

.LONG TERM /THURSDAY THROUGH SUNDAY/...Significant changes in the
pattern begin to take place on Thursday and ensemble clusters are
showing strong support for this now. Broad upper troughing will
take up residence over the interior Western U.S. while relatively
high amplitude ridging offshore starts to build into the Pacific
Northwest. A drying trend will commence on Thursday with the ridge
starting to build into the area. A multi-day warming trend is
expected Friday through Sunday with 500 millibar heights climbing
to near 575 dam or better by Sunday with the ridge axis overhead.
At the same time, the low level flow will turn offshore this
weekend. Combined with 850 millibar temperatures of 12 to 14 C by
Sunday, this would be enough to push quite a few lowland locations
into the 70s. I can hear the lawn mowers already. 27
-- End Changed Discussion --

.MARINE...
-- Changed Discussion --
SCA southerlies remain this morning as an occluded front
drapes just north of the coastal waters. Its associated surface low
will fill and weaken today, allowing for winds to relax and
advisories to expire. The low will push inland on Tuesday bringing
increased onshore flow. Here, an additional round of SCA winds are
favored for the central/eastern strait Tuesday evening. High
pressure then looks to build into the area waters Thursday and
Friday and last into Saturday.

Seas rise to 7 to 9 ft by this evening. They may briefly approach 10
ft by late Monday night before subsiding and hovering around 6-8 ft
through midweek. Easing is expected after that, reaching 3-5 ft
Thursday as surface high pressure builds.

McMillian
