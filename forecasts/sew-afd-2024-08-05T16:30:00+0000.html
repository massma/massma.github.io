.SYNOPSIS...High pressure aloft to start the week with continued
above normal temperatures and dry weather. However, increased
onshore flow will bring near-normal conditions on Tuesday before

marginal thunderstorm potential over the Cascades mainly during
the second half of the week.

.SHORT TERM /TODAY THROUGH WEDNESDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Four Corners ridge remains
in place as its axis is directly over the PNW. Upstream, a baggy
500 mb trough is positioned offshore as its center is just off
the WA/OR coast. Here locally, we`ve been under persistent
southwest flow aloft as the pattern has been rather stagnant so
of course, dry weather and slightly above-normal temperatures for
the interior is expected today. Thunderstorm activity has been
observed so far morning in N OR but SW flow aloft should steer it
clear of our area. Can`t rule out the Lewis County Cascades
getting clipped but confidence is leaning mostly dry. Marine
stratus is making a run inland as morning clouds start things off
but diurnal mixing will give way to mostly clear skies for most
during the afternoon. Highs today are to top into the low to mid
80s across the lowlands with Cascade valleys getting near 90 F.
Coastal areas will maintain in the 60s and 70s.

.LONG TERM /THURSDAY THROUGH SUNDAY/...Deterministic guidance has
quasi-zonal flow of the area on Thursday. Will have to monitor
the continued threat for elevation convection during the second
half of the week. Around Friday, models are hinting at a shortwave
trough over the PNW and upper-ridging upstream over the NE Pac.
Ensemble guidance continues to favor above normal temperatures
through the rest of the forecast period.

McMillian
