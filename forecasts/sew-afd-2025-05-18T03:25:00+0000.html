.SHORT TERM /TONIGHT THROUGH TUESDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Two main areas of precip on
current radar worth monitoring. Over the western half of the CWA,
scattered showers continue to creep slowly southeastward. The other
area is a large convergence zone over much of Snohomish county and
the northern half of King county. Doppler estimated rainfall rates
largely ranging up to 0.3 inches per hour...the highest of which are
mainly present over the Cascade foothills...but there is a slug of
moisture in central King county generating 0.3-0.5 inches per hour.
Thankfully, this activity has missed lingering burn scars, but this
will need to be monitored. Hi-res guidance continues to show this
feature lingering into tonight before finally fizzling out early
Sunday morning. Any lightning associated with precip has remained
well out of the CWA and with the loss of the sun, should be able to
put that threat to bed.

Inherited forecast handles the above well and thus no need for an
evening update at this time. For additional forecast details, please
refer to the Previous Discussion section below.
