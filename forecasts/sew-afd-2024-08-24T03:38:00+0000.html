.SYNOPSIS...Cool and unusually wet weather will linger into
Saturday followed by drier more seasonal weather Sunday. Another
shot of unsettled weather will brush the area Monday night into
Tuesday, followed by a return of drier warmer pattern with near
normal temperatures through the latter half of next week.

&&

.SHORT TERM /TONIGHT THROUGH MONDAY/...A very moist air mass -
more like October than August remains in place over the region as
an unusually deep upper level low works slowly through the Pacific
Northwest. Have included a low-end chance of thunder over much of
the interior lowlands this evening, supported by NBM
probabilities and the presence of the upper level low and
embedded shortwaves rotating out of it. The best bet for isolated
thunder continues to be over the Cascades this afternoon into the
NBM where HREF 6 hour probabilities peak out in the 40-60% chance
range along the north Cascade crest. Otherwise, widespread light
rain and low clouds will be the norm this evening for most areas,
with afternoon temperatures remaining in the upper 50s.

The upper level low will weaken and meander eastward tonight with
easterly flow aloft. This will allow any evening thunderstorms
over the Cascades to slip west over the foothills before
weakening. Larger scale dynamics look to help keep more widespread
precipitation over the central and northern portion of the area
late tonight into Saturday morning. The NBM continues to show over
80% probabilities of 2 or more inches possible over the northern
Cascades through Saturday morning. Will have to keep an eye on
the Central and Northern Cascade burn scars especially with
possible higher short-period rain rates. Widespread, heavier
precipitation will weaken as the upper level trough move east
Saturday leaving scattered showers in it`s wake Saturday afternoon
- especially over the Cascades.

Increasing onshore flow behind the trough will also make for a
breezy day especially for areas adjacent to the Strait of Juan de
Fuca. Skies will remain cloudy with highs again well below normal
- in the lower to mid 60s. Conditions will trend drier Saturday
night with an upper level ridge building into the area. Low level
onshore flow will keep skies at least mostly cloudy into Sunday
morning, with some sunshine in the afternoon and highs in the mid
60s to lower 70s.

.LONG TERM /TUESDAY THROUGH FRIDAY/...Extended models in good
agreement with another progressive upper level trough moving into
Western Washington Monday night into Tuesday. Another round of
cooler, wetter weather. The grand ensemble continues to highlight
large scale ridging settling into the region midweek and possibly into
the following weekend. This would at least highlight a trend to
more normal temperatures through the later half of next week and
at least August typical sunshine.

.MARINE...
-- Changed Discussion --
A low pressure system will gradually push inland on
Saturday. A weak frontal system then looks to move into the area
waters on Saturday, which will be followed by yet another system
pushing into the area waters on Monday. High pressure will then
gradually build back into the northeastern Pacific Tuesday and
persist through midweek.

Winds along the Strait will peak tonight into early Saturday. Latest
guidance continues to show southerly winds increasing across the
interior waters tonight as well, so the Small Craft Advisories for
the Puget Sound, Admiralty Inlet, and Northern Inland Waters for
frequent gusts to 25-30 kt will remain in effect, which are expected
to persist through Saturday morning.

Seas across the coastal waters will generally persist at 3-6 ft
through the weekend and into early next week.

Unusually wet conditions are expected through
