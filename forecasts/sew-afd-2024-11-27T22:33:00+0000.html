.SHORT TERM /TONIGHT THROUGH SATURDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Current radar shows some
light isolated showers moving through portions of the region.
Precipitation chances should decrease into the evening. Partly to
mostly cloudy skies tonight. Low level moisture and light winds
will bring another round of patchy fog possible in the southern
interior on Thursday morning. On Thanksgiving Day, northerly winds
will advect drier air into the region, limiting precipitation
chances along most of Western Washington. NBM guidance is showing
some chances of precipitation in northern Olympic Peninsula (20%
to 30%). However, any precipitation that does occur will be light.

A weak disturbance will move south on Friday from British Columbia
bringing precipitation chances and slightly cooler air to the
region. Higher PoPs are limited mainly to the northern Olympic
Peninsula and northern Cascades (30% to 50%). Any precipitation
that does occur will be light and should pose no significant
impacts. Precipitation chances will decrease as Friday progresses
and Saturday should remain relatively dry. High pressure building
in the Pacific will gradually begin to shift east and settle
over the area on Saturday.

Mostly to partly cloudy skies are expected to continue throughout
the week. High temperatures will be in the 40s. Low temperatures
will be in the 30s, with the coldest spots being in the southern
interior.

.LONG TERM /SUNDAY THROUGH WEDNESDAY/...The upper ridge will
continue to build across the Pacific Northwest on Sunday allowing
for continued dry conditions, with areas of fog in the morning.
Ensembles begin to diverge a bit early next week in regards to the
magnitude of ridging, or if the ridge flattens allowing for more SW
flow aloft. NBM begins to introduce precip chances on Monday,
although only approximately 25% of ensemble members suggest
measurable QPF through 00z Tuesday. Otherwise, continued uncertainty
exists Tuesday and Wednesday with further increase in precipitation
probabilities due to a more SW flow component aloft, despite a
continued ridging influence. Temperatures will be slightly below
normal on Sunday and a few degrees above normal the middle of next
week. JD
