.SHORT TERM /TODAY THROUGH SATURDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Clear skies prevail across
Western Washington this morning with upper ridging in control and
weak offshore flow near the surface. A thermal trough is oriented
along the coast today and this will give coastal areas their
warmest day of the week. Most of the interior lowlands away from
the water will warm several degrees today as well...from near 80
north to lower 90s in the Southwest Interior. The thermal trough
axis will shift into the interior lowlands on Friday. Coastal
areas will cool 10 or more degrees on Friday, but interior
locations will remain warm. The overall pattern doesn`t change too
much heading into Saturday. Heights fall a smidge, but onshore
flow doesn`t look strong enough to significantly cool interior
areas.

.LONG TERM /SUNDAY THROUGH WEDNESDAY/...Moving into early next
week, strong upper ridging centered over the Four Corners region
flattens slightly and weak upper troughing just offshore moves
closer to the region. Despite this subtle shift in the synoptic
pattern, 500 millibar heights remain relatively high...north of
580 dam. Slightly lower heights and a little more onshore flow may
bring some slight cooling, but above normal temperatures and dry
conditions look to continue. The most significant uncertainty in
the extended forecast period lies with any embedded disturbances
in upper troughing that could lead to some thunderstorm activity
over the Cascades. Only a minority of the ensembles show much in
the way of convective possibilities early next week, but it`s
definitely worth watching for fire weather interests. 27
-- End Changed Discussion --
