.SHORT TERM /TODAY THROUGH MONDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Satellite imagery shows
mostly clear skies over Western Washington at 3 am/10z.
Temperature dew point spreads getting near zero in the South Puget
Sound but not fog being reported. Temperatures mostly in the upper
30s to mid 40s.

Upper level ridge remaining over the area today stalling a weather
system offshore. Forecast will have some cloud cover in it but the
clouds will be high level clouds. Patchy fog in the South Puget
Sound will be gone by mid morning. With the front stalling
offshore, a little offshore flow in the morning and warm south
southwesterly flow aloft highs today will be well above normal, in
the 60s and lower 70s. While no record highs are expected the
forecast high of 69 in Seattle would tie for the 4th warmest April
5th in 81 years.

Upper level ridge shifting east of the area tonight but with the
parent low associated with the offshore front moving north the
front will not be making much eastward progress overnight. Rain
out ahead of the front reaching the coast in the early morning
hours. Increasing middle level clouds for the remainder of the
area will result in warmer low temperatures versus this morning,
in the mid 40s to lower 50s.

Rain slowly spreading inland Sunday reaching the the Puget Sound
area early in the afternoon and the Cascades by late afternoon.
Elevated snow levels, 6500 to 7500 feet, so just rain in the
passes and Olympics. Parent low for the front in the Gulf of
Alaska with little surface gradient over the area. Winds not much
of a factor. A little gusty in the usual spots like Whidbey
Island. Highs lowering to the mid and upper 50s.

Front moving through Sunday night with rain all areas. Snow
levels remaining elevated keeping the precipitation type as rain
in the passes. Lows in the mid and upper 40s.

Upper level trough swinging into the area Monday keeping showers
in the forecast. Increasing onshore gradients with the trough in
the afternoon could produce some breezy conditions ( not great
for the cherry blossoms ). Highs in the mid 50s.

.LONG TERM /TUESDAY THROUGH FRIDAY/...Quick moving system pushed
along by a 130-140 knot jet across the Eastern Pacific arriving
Tuesday evening with rain ahead of the front during the day
Tuesday. Jet moving inland over Central Oregon so Western
Washington not getting the strongest portion of the system but
still enough to give the area another round of rain.

Upper level ridge trying to build in the wake of the system
Wednesday but the ridge not looking strong enough at this point to
keep a chance of showers out of the forecast.

GFS and ECMWF ensembles in good agreement with another fast
moving system being pushed by a 100 knot plus jet moving into
Western Washington Thursday. Like the previous system an upper
level ridge will try and build behind the front Friday. Ensembles
not very impressed with the ridge. Both models have precipitation
on at least half of the ensemble solutions. This will keep a
chance of showers in the forecast.

High temperatures near or slight above normal through the period.
Felton
-- End Changed Discussion --

.MARINE...
-- Changed Discussion --
Surface high pressure remaining over area waters through
early Sunday. However, The next frontal system will move through the
waters as Sunday progress, bringing a quick round of breezy winds
and high seas. A SCA has been issued for the coastal waters Sunday
morning through Monday afternoon. Additional systems will enter area
waters through next week as the pattern re-amplifies. Breezy winds
and elevated seas are in the forecast.

Combined seas 4 to 6 feet today. Seas will start to gradually build
towards 8 to 10 feet tonight into Sunday, reaching 10 to 12 feet by
Sunday afternoon-evening and remaining in the 8 to 12 ft range
through Tuesday.

McMillian
