.SHORT TERM /TODAY THROUGH SATURDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Dry, northerly, flow sets
up over WA today with high pressure centered offshore (near
130W). Clearing skies have allowed temps to dip into the 30s and
lower 40s early this morning with freezing temps around the south
sound. Near the surface, there`s patchy low clouds and fog in the
interior that will burn off through the late morning. Expect
plenty of sun this afternoon with highs in the low to mid 50s.

The ridge shifts inland Friday and Saturday for more dry and mild
weather. The air mass will be a little warmer with highs on
Saturday reaching the upper 50s to lower 60s. Morning lows will
still be chilly and in the 30s. 33

.LONG TERM /SUNDAY THROUGH WEDNESDAY/...The ridge flattens a bit
by Sunday and shifts a little east and farther inland. This now
brings rain to the coast, Olympic peninsula and North Interior as
a weak front tries to shift in. Rain increases toward the evening
for Seattle south as the front slowly pushes farther in. Snow
levels hover around 4,000 ft then drop to around 2,500-3,000 late
Sunday night. The north Cascades may see heavy snow during this
period with around 1-3" at the passes.

We`re under moist onshore flow on Monday with additional showers
and mountain snow. More wet weather is headed our way by Tuesday
with a passing short wave trough. A stronger system approaches the
region toward midweek bringing the potential for windier weather.

.MARINE...
-- Changed Discussion --
A SCA remains in effect for high seas through Thursday
evening for the outer coastal waters. Otherwise, high pressure will
maintain calmer marine conditions through Friday. Then, a frontal
system will move over the waters late Friday night into the weekend,
bringing small craft winds to the coastal waters, Strait of Juan de
Fuca, and Northern Interior waters. Seas 8 to 10 ft today,
decreasing to 6 to 8 ft tonight through Friday. Seas will build to
10-14 ft Friday night through the weekend as an active weather
pattern is favored into next week.
