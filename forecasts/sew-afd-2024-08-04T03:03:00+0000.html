From Previous Discussion...Latest satellite imagery shows increasing
high level clouds over western Washington as monsoonal moisture is
bring brought northeastward around a large upper level ridge
centered over the Four Corners area. Some marine stratus persists
along the immediate coastline as well. Temperatures across the
region are in the 70s, with 60s along the Pacific coast. Conditions
will remain dry through the short term period as the ridge remains
parked in place. Temperatures will remain above average, with highs
in the mid 70s to upper 80s and lows in the mid 50s to low 60s.
Sunday will likely be the warmest day in this period. Monday will
bring cooler temperatures to the coast as a trough begins to move
into the region. The trough pass through the region on Tuesday,
bringing some relief with temperatures in the 70s to near 80, which
is just about normal for this time of year. Though temperatures are
above normal, HeatRisk values remain Moderate/Orange through the
interior north of Seattle, and Minor/Yellow elsewhere. Additionally,
in this pattern, there will be a very slight (10-15% probability)
for a few isolated showers, which could produce a few lightning
strikes, to develop along the crest of the North Cascades.
Confidence is fairly low at this point, however, impacts are not
expected to be significant.

.LONG TERM /WEDNESDAY THROUGH SATURDAY/...From Previous
Discussion...Models are hinting at another warming trend through the
end of next week as the trough departs and heights rise in its wake.
Temperatures look to top out into the upper 80s to low 90s by
Thursday. Another upper level low then looks to drifts southward out
of Alberta/BC towards the end of the week, which may bring
temperatures down closer to normal and slight chances for
precipitation.
