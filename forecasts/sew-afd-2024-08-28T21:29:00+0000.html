.SHORT TERM /TONIGHT THROUGH SUNDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Mostly clear skies are in
place across western Washington this afternoon. The upper level
trough that brought active weather to the region yesterday has
moved off to the east while ridging begins to build along the
Pacific coast. This ridging will result in dry conditions and a
gradual warming trend that will last in the weekend. This warming
should also reduce the coverage/intensity of morning fog each
morning.

By Saturday, expect highs generally in the upper 70s and 80s
across western Washington. This will result in HeatRisk levels
reaching moderate/orange across many areas. The highest HeatRisk
levels are expected in the Cascade valleys where very isolated
major HeatRisk is possible. At this time, conditions appear
unlikely to warrant any heat products, but we will continue to
monitor the forecast. Warm conditions will continue into Sunday as
the ridge axis slides east of the area.

.LONG TERM /MONDAY THROUGH WEDNESDAY/...By Monday, a compact but
relatively weak upper level low meandering around the eastern
Pacific will get ejected northeast somewhere between northern CA
and southern WA Monday into Tuesday. This will allow cooler
temperatures and a slight chance of precipitation to return to the
area. At this time, there is not a robust signal for significant
precipitation/convection, however again - it`s something we`ll be
monitoring.

Confidence in the details of the pattern decrease significantly
by the middle of next week. However, extended outlooks from our
colleagues at the Climate Prediction Center strongly favor above
normal temperatures and a weak signal for below normal
precipitation.
-Wolcott-
-- End Changed Discussion --

.FIRE WEATHER...
-- Changed Discussion --
High pressure will begin to develop east of the
Cascades on Thursday with a thermal trough along the coast. This
will allow winds to turn light offshore over the Cascades starting
Thursday. This will allow relative humidity values to drop into the
30s, even as low as 20-25% possible in the southern parts of the
Cascades and valleys. Relative humidity recovery will be poor
overnight, only reaching around 40-60%. Area fuels are currently
moist from recent rains, but these conditions will allow them to dry
out quickly. It will be important to monitor how fast fuels respond,
especially as temperatures warm through late week and instability
looks to increase into the holiday weekend.

LH
