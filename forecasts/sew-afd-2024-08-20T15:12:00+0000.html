.SHORT TERM /TODAY THROUGH THURSDAY/...Latest NWS radar imagery
depicts several bands of precipitation spreading onshore across
the region, rotating broadly around the parent low that has
remained offshore for the last day or so. Guidance continues to
suggest this low will finally push onshore through the day, with
the southerly flow bringing moisture and the colder air aloft
steepening lapse rates. This will maintain plenty of showers and
perhaps a few isolated thunderstorms as the low tracks eastward
today. The main threats will be brief downpours, lightning, and
small hail, along with gusty winds. As the colder air moves in
behind the front, expect temperatures to largely trend some 5 to
10 degrees cooler than yesterday. The next low dropping from the
Gulf of Alaska will quickly slide down the Pacific coast from
near southern Vancouver Island Wednesday to the Oregon/California
border by late Thursday. Additional showers and isolated thunderstorms
remain possible as this system moves by, and temperatures will
remain cooler than normal.

.LONG TERM /FRIDAY THROUGH MONDAY/...Expect the low well to the
south to slide eastward into the Great Basin, providing for some
slightly rising heights over the region. However, a few embedded
disturbances likely provide the focus for more rain at times into
the first half of the weekend. Ensemble guidance then slightly
favors a shift back to a warmer and drier pattern for the early
portions of next week. However, still plenty of variability in
potential outcomes and confidence in the specifics remains lower
for the weekend and beyond at this point.
