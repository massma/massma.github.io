.SYNOPSIS...High pressure aloft will gradually build through the

.SHORT TERM /TODAY THROUGH FRIDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Forecast remains on track
this morning with no updates required. Mostly clear skies in
place over W WA this early morning as the area finds itself more
under the influence of an upper level high over BC as opposed to
the weak troughiness to the south. As this high remains fairly
stationary for the remainder of the work week, dry conditions will
persist with warming temperatures through Friday. Daytime highs
today will get into the mid to upper 70s for most of the interior
lowlands while the coast and most water adjacent areas will see
highs in the mid 60s to right around 70. Surface level high
pressure over southern BC will set up an offshore flow pattern
Thursday, further boosting temperatures in what will be the
warmest day all around with most lowlands seeing highs in the mid
70s to lower 80s. Start seeing hints of the return to onshore flow
starting Friday, as coastal and water adjacent locations return
to the mid 60s to around 70 while the interior remains toasty with
highs once again in the mid 70s to lower 80s.

While the majority of the CWA will be dry, it is worth mentioning
that some lingering moisture and instability over the
Cascades...particularly the North Cascades, will still run the risk
for some afternoon and evening scattered showers and possibly some
isolated thunderstorms. Rainfall with any of these showers or
thunderstorms is expected to be generally light.

Lastly, this discussion of warming temperatures once again turns
attention to heat risk potential. Even with the high temperatures
mentioned above, overnight lows will continue to cool down into the
lower to mid 50s throughout the area. While there are pockets of
moderate HeatRisk...mainly confined to the Seattle metro area and
some of the Cascade valleys, current data suggests widespread heat
impacts are expected to be minimal at this time.

.LONG TERM /SATURDAY THROUGH TUESDAY/...An incoming frontal system
associated with an upper level low dipping south from the Canadian
coast hints at a little bit of a pattern change for the weekend and
the beginning of next week as the low mostly sits and churns in
place, pushing at least a couple of fronts through the area. This
will take a degree or two off daytime highs Saturday but will knock
temps back into the 60s for most locations Sunday and Monday...with
some recovery back to the lower 70s Tuesday. PoPs during this time
frame are less than impressive however, with most of the CWA only
seeing chances ranging from the single digits to around 20-30 pct
Monday morning. Instead, the coast and northern tier of the CWA
appear to be favored, where PoPs there will peak in the 40 to 50 pct
range both Sunday and Monday. Tuesday sees activity starting to wind
up...but PoPs will still linger in the 10-20 pct range for the
aforementioned favored locations.
