.SHORT TERM /TONIGHT THROUGH SATURDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Moist, S/SW diffluent flow
is over western WA as a trough digs offshore. We`re seeing off-
and-on showers with snow in the mountains. Snow levels will hover
around 4000 ft this afternoon, then drop to around 1500-2000 ft
by Thursday morning. The Cascades and passes will see accumulating
snow with a batch of heavier precipitation moving in tonight into
early Thursday.

The air mass will remain cool and showery on Thursday as a trough
moves inland. Temps will be chilly with morning lows in the 30s
and highs in the 40s. The air mass is slightly unstable in
SW WA where there`s a chance of afternoon thunderstorms.

More cool and wet weather is on tap for Friday as another short
wave trough tracks through. The bulk of the energy and moisture is
to our south with mainly light snowfall accumulations expected in
the mountains. Temps are still tracking cooler than average with
lows in the lower to mids 30s and highs mostly in the 40s.

A steadier stream of moisture is aimed at WA this weekend with the
jet overhead. Good orographics indicated in the models for another
round of potentialy heavy snow in the mountains. May also see
windy/breezy conditions with the surface low, especially along the
coast. 33

.LONG TERM /SUNDAY THROUGH WEDNESDAY/...Active weather continues
on Sunday with more rain, mountain snow and breezy/windy
conditions. Heavy snow is still expected in the Cascades where
storm total snow amounts could total up to 2 feet. Heavy
precipitation in the Olympics may also drive the Skokomish River
in Mason county into flood stage. The moisture plume gets cut off
Sunday night as a trough moves in (pushing the band toward our
south). This would end the heavy precip threat for both lowlands
and mountains. Monday will see additional showers with troughing
over the West. Ensemble members show a drier period moving toward
midweek as a ridge moves in. 33
-- End Changed Discussion --

.AVIATION...
-- Changed Discussion --
Variable conditions across the Western Washington as
rain continues to push from south to north. A few breaks in
precipitation and cloud cover have allowed improvement into VFR.
Ceilings will lower later this afternoon/evening into MVFR as more
abundant cloud cover and more rain showers push through the
region. Localized IFR is possible in heavier bands of rain.
Southerly/southeasterly winds will shift northerly/northwesterly
later this afternoon (after 0Z). Winds will then shift
southwesterly between 10-12Z and increase in speed, primarily
along Puget Sound terminals and the Strait of Juan de Fuca (KCLM),
with speeds of 10- 15 kt and gusts up to 20-25 kt.

afternoon into MVFR as lower clouds and more rain push north from
