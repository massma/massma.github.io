A band of snow showers stemming from Aberdeen towards

.SHORT TERM /TODAY THROUGH SATURDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Overall, the main
determination for precipitation type through early this morning
will continue to be the temperature profile, so will be monitoring
this closely over the next few hours. Winter weather advisories
remain in effect through late this morning for much of the
interior lowlands as an additional inch of snow will still be
possible in any heavier precipitation bands that do develop.

Hi-res guidance indicates shower activity tapering by the
afternoon hours as the low offshore sinks southward. Clearing
skies this afternoon and evening will bring significant cooling to
the region by Friday morning, with many lowland locations
expecting to see overnight lows dip into the teens and 20s. Cold
Weather Advisories remain in effect for the Cascade foothills and
the Puget Sound lowlands extending from King County up through
Whatcom county through Friday morning.

Chances for snow showers will increase again late Friday into
Saturday morning as the upper low moves inland to the south and
pushes some wrap around moisture back up into the region. A weak
shortwave will follow close behind, dropping into the region from
the north and will likely keep shower activity going across
western Washington through Saturday. While confidence in regards
to exact snow amounts and intensity of showers remains low at this
time, amounts at this time generally look to be lighter. Temperatures
into the weekend will continue to trend below average, with highs
on Saturday in the upper 30s to low 40s and overnight lows
expected to be in the mid 20s to near freezing.

.LONG TERM /SUNDAY THROUGH WEDNESDAY/...Western Washington will
remain under northerly flow aloft as upper troughing looks to
persist over the Pacific Northwest and upper level ridging builds
over the northeastern Pacific and Gulf of Alaska in the long term.
Temperatures will remain below normal and look to cool heading
into midweek, with highs expected to be in the 30s and overnight
lows consistently dipping down into the 20s and teens. Weak systems
embedded in the northerly flow aloft may bring additional chances
for snow showers at times through early next week.
