.SHORT TERM /TONIGHT THROUGH WEDNESDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Western Washington is
seeing some breaks in the clouds this afternoon, but are unlikely
to scatter completely into the evening hours. Another round of
stratus clouds will arrive to start the day on Monday, eventually
breaking out for a high temperature in the mid to upper 70s as the
ridge moves closer to the coast. Temperatures on Tuesday have
backed off slightly, likely due to more onshore flow, but a day
in the upper 70s and even 80 in the valleys is likely.

A weak frontal system will drop in from the north on Wednesday,
introducing PoPs to the coast and north interior, spreading
showers south and east into late Wednesday night. Very light
rainfall, on the order of a few hundredths of an inch, are
possible across much of the area into Thursday. Temperatures will
cool down the upper 60s.

.LONG TERM /THURSDAY THROUGH SUNDAY/...Another few shortwave
systems progress through the region Thursday into Friday for
isolated shower activity. By Friday afternoon, any lingering
activity should be confined to the central and northern Cascades.

By the weekend, what looks like a developing Four Corners high
pressure system could begin to take shape, and as it amplifies,
contributes to warming over the PNW. Highs on Saturday climb into
the mid 70s, and again on Sunday into the low 80s. As with all
possibilities with significant lead time, exact details will
continue to evolve.

.AVIATION...
-- Changed Discussion --
Northerly flow aloft continues as upper troughing
moves eastward with weak ridging offshore. Mostly VFR/MVFR across
the terminals this afternoon as conditions gradually begin to dry
out. Conditions will continue to improve throughout the evening,
likely rebounding to VFR after 00z. With onshore flow increasing
tonight, stratus will spread inland towards the interior for
another round of MVFR conditions, even potentially some localized
IFR conditions as well. S/SW winds throughout this evening
remaining around 4 to 8 knots.

KSEA...VFR/MVFR conditions early this afternoon will settle into
VFR by 00z. W/SW winds around 5 to 10 knots this afternoon,
decreasing to 3 to 6 knots overnight and into Monday morning.
Marine stratus will spread inland overnight, likely reaching the
terminal by 13z-16z, with a 30% chance of MVFR conditions through
the early morning. Stratus will scatter out and rebound to VFR by
the afternoon.

.MARINE...
