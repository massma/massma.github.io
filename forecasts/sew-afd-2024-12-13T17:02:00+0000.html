Current radar imagery already showing some light to moderate rain
starting to move along the Pacific Coast of Washington as a low
pressure system spins in the Pacific Ocean.

A strong weather system is expected to impact Western Washington
on Friday morning through Sunday. This system will bring gusty
winds, lowland rain, mountain snow, and high surf to the region.
Coastal flooding is expected to impact the shorelines throughout
the weekend. Details on hazards expected are outlined below:

LOWLAND RAIN: Rain will start to move into the coast in the
morning hours of Friday and become widespread throughout the day.
There is a chance of isolated thunderstorms on Saturday,
along the coast, with a 15-20% chance. The main hazards with these
thunderstorms will be: gusty winds, small hail, brief heavy
downpours, and lighting. The heaviest rainfall will be along the
coast and the Olympic Peninsula. The rain should begin to taper
off on Sunday morning.

MOUNTAIN SNOW: Snow levels on Friday will range between 3000 to
3500 ft on Friday and decrease to 2000 to 2500 ft by Sunday.
Expect snow at the Olympics and over the Cascades. As of now,
currently expected 6 to 8 inches at Stevens Pass and 4 to 6
inches at Snoqualmie Pass. There is some uncertainty on the
snowfall totals at Snoqualmie Pass due to the easterly winds
impacting snow levels. As a result, higher localized snowfall
amounts are possible if the easterly winds keep the snow levels
lower.

WINDS: Winds are expected to increase on Friday afternoon and
eventually subside on Saturday evening. The strongest winds will
be along the coast, the San Juan Islands, Whidbey Island, and western
Skagit and Whatcom counties. Maximum wind gusts between 30 to 40
mph are possible at the locations.

COASTAL FLOODING: Coastal flooding is expected along the Pacific
Coast on Friday. The flooding threat will persist for the outer
coast through the weekend and will gradually move inland through
the weekend. Flooding is expected during the late morning to mid-
day high tides. Significant coastal flooding will be possible
along the Pacific Coast on Saturday with an inundation of 2-3 ft
above ground level possible. Large waves building along the
Pacific coast could exacerbate impacts to coastal communities.

HIGH SURF: Large waves will build towards 17 to 19 feet at around
along the Pacific Coast on Saturday. Large waves may wash over
beaches, jetties, and other structures unexpectedly. Localized
beach erosion will be possible.

.LONG TERM /MONDAY THROUGH THURSDAY/...Wet and active conditions
seem to continue through Wednesday as a couple of systems are
expected to move through Western Washington bringing lowland rain
and mountain snow. Ensemble guidance is hinting at an upper level
ridge building over the region and potentially bringing drier
conditions on Thursday. However there is uncertainty on whether it
will completely dry out as the GFS is showing some disturbances
in the flow, potentially keeping the chance of precipitation to
remain in the forecast.

.MARINE...
-- Changed Discussion --
A sub 990mb surface low currently resides offshore. This
system will track towards the region today through Saturday with
impacts. A Gale warning is in place for the coastal waters during
this time. Occasional gusts to storm force are possible through the
outer coastal waters tonight into early Saturday morning.
Additionally, gale force can`t completely be ruled out over the
Northern Inland waters and East Strait into Saturday morning. At the
least, SCA may be needed for the Northern Inland waters along with
other inland water zones for Saturday, as well as Admiralty Inlet,
Puget Sound, and Hood Canal. Currently, a Small Craft Advisory is in
effect for the entire Strait of Juan de Fuca through early Saturday
morning.

We`ll get a break in the action as high pressure builds over the
region on Sunday. Then, another frontal system will move through the
area on Monday but much weaker than what we`ll see this weekend.
Breezy winds may continue to bring headlines as we start the new
week.

Big seas expected through Saturday as 10 to 12 ft waves increase to
16 to 20 this evening. Seas could get up to 23 ft for the outer
coastal waters on Saturday. Seas should subside below 10 ft on
Sunday but look to increase again well over 10 ft to start the new
week.

.HYDROLOGY...A stronger storm will set up along the coast over
the weekend with the potential to bring more significant
precipitation to the region. Latest guidance has been uncertain
regarding rainfall amounts in the Olympics, with overall totals
being lower than originally forecasted. Area rivers will rise,
with only the Skokomish River being expected to reach above action
stage. Right now, the forecast did lower, with the river hovering
just under minor flood stage Saturday into early Sunday. This
will continued to be monitored as more guidance becomes available.
Additional rounds of precipitation could keep the Skokomish river
at or near flood stage into early next week. We will continue to
monitor the rainfall forecast and the river forecast for this
weekend.
