.SHORT TERM /TODAY THROUGH WEDNESDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
A weak front and
associated upper trough is spreading a few showers into Western
Washington early this morning. This activity will wind down by
afternoon as the disturbance shifts east of the Cascades. High
temperatures will cool a couple more degrees over yesterday. The
downturn will be brief as upper ridging rebuilds into the area
tonight and Tuesday. Temperatures will bounce back several
degrees as thermally induced low pressure expands northward
along the coast and the low level flow turns weakly offshore.
The thermal trough will shift inland on Wednesday with
temperatures climbing further over the interior. Widespread
highs in the 70s to mid 80s are expected on Wednesday away from
the immediate coast.
-- End Changed Discussion --

-- Changed Discussion --
The upper ridge
axis moves into the Northern Rockies on Thursday with another
