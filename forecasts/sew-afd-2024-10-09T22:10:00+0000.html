.SYNOPSIS...
-- Changed Discussion --
A few lingering showers will continue to trail a
departing disturbance this afternoon across most of western
Washington. The pattern will become drier and warmer as upper
level ridging builds into the region from Thursday into the
weekend. Expect the pattern to transition back to cool and
unsettled by middle by next week as the ridging is replaced with a
trough.
-- End Changed Discussion --

.SHORT TERM /TONIGHT THROUGH SATURDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
A weak upper level trough
is sliding north over far northern Washington/B.C. Canada this
afternoon. There is just enough dynamical support a few isolated
showers continue post-front off the coast up into Vancouver
Island. These will continue to move inland and affect areas
primarily north of I-90 and the Olympics. Additionally, an
isolated thunderstorm off the coastline cannot be ruled out
(lightning has been detected off the coastline off of Neah Bay but
but the thunder is not expected to make it inland). Precipitation
amounts are extremely light for today (several hundredths inland
to over a tenth along the coast). Places that do not see showers
this afternoon will see intervals of clouds and sunshine, as the
high cirrostratus/cirrocumulus clouds move out. Temperatures will
settle in the upper 50s to low 60s this afternoon.

The remainder of this week into the weekend looks dry and pleasant
without any weather makers passing over our region. Flow aloft
will become more zonal with the jet pattern up in Canada Thursday
and Friday, building into a ridge over Washington and B.C. as a
split low cuts underneath, and a low deepens off of Alaska. As a
result, the weather Thursday into Saturday will be completely dry.
Thursday afternoon and late Friday into Saturday appear to be the
sunniest days in the forecast. In between, a mix of clouds will
block out some of the clear sky. Additionally, there will be the
potential for some patchy fog each morning with weak flow aloft,
partially clear skies in the morning and light winds. The warmest
temperatures will arrive by Friday/Saturday, as highs in most
areas will peak in the upper 60s to low 70s. This will put most
places around 5 to 10 degrees above normal temperatures (for this
time in October).

.LONG TERM /SUNDAY THROUGH WEDNESDAY/...Sunday continues the
pleasant weather from Saturday as the ridge is expected to peak in
its build over the region Sunday morning. Most ensembles are
showing the ridge beginning to exit the region by Monday/Tuesday
(though this may speed up or slow depending on if a low develops
over the Great Lakes). Western Washington will most likely see the
return of cooler and wetter conditions Monday into Wednesday, as
a trough from Alaska begins to track towards our area.
Predictability of precipitation amounts with this system remain
challenging to pinpoint this far out, but it appears most areas
will receive a modest amount of precipitation next week.

HPR
