.SHORT TERM /TODAY THROUGH TUESDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Satellite, obs and radar all
indicating life under a winter ridge for the most part, with
generally quiet conditions, widespread low clouds and some patchy
fog here and there. Current radar does show a mass of showers over
the Admiralty Inlet area that is creeping southward with the trend
actually holding it together pretty well. Switching to satellite, we
see a line of mid to high clouds stretching in a narrow NNW to SSE
line crossing the Sound. Taking this data together and merging it
with models, this is all likely an indication for forecasted
shortwaves moving southward along the leading edge of the upper
level ridge just off to the west. As the day progresses and the
ridge axis leans into W WA, skies will gradually clear and any light
showers will fizzle out.

As ridging intensifies over the region throughout the start of this
week, the pattern will remain generally dry with a persistent risk
for widespread overnight and early morning patchy fog or, at the
very least, some low stratus. The expectation is that these
conditions will lift and burn off by late morning/early afternoon,
although timing will vary from day to day.

Temps will not vary much during the near term, with daytime highs
generally running in the mid to upper 40s today and Tuesday,
although Monday will be slightly cooler with highs in the lower to
mid 40s. Overnight lows will be quite brisk, dipping to around
freezing and bottoming out in the lower to mid 30s.

.LONG TERM /WEDNESDAY THROUGH SATURDAY/...From Previous
Discussion...Expect some increased cloud cover late Wednesday into
Thursday as ensemble guidance continues to support a brief shake-up
in the pattern as the next upper trough slides into south-central
British Columbia. Still some discrepancies in both the depth of this
trough and exactly what path it will take. Unsurprisingly, PoPs
still hold around 20 percent, highest in the north, and the bigger
sensible weather impact for most will be the additional clouds and a
bit more mixing which should bring a brief reprieve from the fog and
any increasingly stagnant air. For the remainder of the week, the
spread in potential scenarios becomes increasingly large owing
primarily to differences in the position and amplitude of the
Pacific ridge - specifically , is it close enough to block the
region from additional disturbances or far enough offshore to leave
the door open. One thing to keep an eye on with future cycles of
guidance in the potential for even colder air to arrive around next
weekend, with the forecast trending a bit cooler late next weekend.
Cullen
