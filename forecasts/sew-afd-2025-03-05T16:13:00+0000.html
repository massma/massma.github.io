.SHORT TERM /TODAY THROUGH FRIDAY/...There is weak upper level
troughing over the Pac NW today as the jet dives south into
southern CA. Across western WA, we`re seeing a few
lingering/isolated showers on radar with this trend continuing
through the day. There`s been some clearing overnight which has
allowed temps to drop into the 30s (mainly around the south
sound). Temperatures this afternoon will reach the lower 50s or
close to normal.

Dry, north, flow sets up on Thursday as a ridge nudges into the
West. The drier air mass will knock temps further down with lows
near freezing around the south sound. We may see patchy morning
low clouds and/or fog, otherwise clear skies by the afternoon.

The ridge shifts inland on Friday for another dry and stable
weather day. Morning lows again will be cool and in the 30s.
Expect highs around 50. 33

.LONG TERM /SATURDAY THROUGH TUESDAY/...High pressure dominates
the West this weekend, keeping precipitation to our north and into
southern B.C. The air mass will be warmer with highs in the 50s to
lower 60s both Saturday and Sunday. Wetter weather returns Sunday
night and Monday as a weak frontal system slowly shifts inland.
Snow levels hover around 3500 ft with a few inches of snow on the
higher mountain peaks and passes. Moist, onshore flow follows on
Tuesday for additional lowland rain and mountain snow. 33
