.SHORT TERM /TODAY THROUGH THURSDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Upper level ridge over the
area the last couple of days flattening today as a weak shortwave
rides by to the north. Not much with this feature for Western
Washington other than some increasing clouds over the northern
portions later today. Lots of places have temperature dew point
spreads of 1 degree or less so still a chance for patchy freezing
fog to develop before sunrise. What little that does form will be
gone pretty quickly once the sun comes up. Highs today in the
lower to mid 40s.

Upper level ridge rebuilding tonight into Wednesday. With the
clear skies overnight temperatures will once again drop into the
20s in many locations. Patchy freezing fog possible in the usual
fog prone locations like the Southwest Interior. Sunny skies
Wednesday. Highs continuing to be a couple of degrees below
normal, in the lower to mid 40s.

Another shortwave will move over the top of the ridge Wednesday
night. This feature will be just north of Vancouver Island by 12z
Thursday. Far enough north to keep skies over Western Washington
mostly clear overnight leading to another morning with lows
mostly in the 20s.

Upper level ridge retrograding slightly Thursday with the flow
aloft turning northwesterly. This will push the shortwave towards
Western Washington during the day but the feature will still be
north of the area by 00z Friday. Increasing clouds mainly in the
late morning and afternoon hours over the northern portions with
a slight chance of rain near the Canadian border in the afternoon.
Highs a little cooler with the increasing cloud cover, mostly in
the lower 40s.

Operational model runs differing on how much of the shortwave is
left as it moves into Western Washington Thursday night. As the
feature approaches the low level flow will turn weakly onshore.
Both the GFS and ECMWF keep 1000-850 mb thickness values above
1300 meters ( although not by much over Whatcom county ). Model
snow levels right now in the 1500-2000 foot range. Given the
pattern this looks a little high but with the low level onshore
flow snow levels should stay off the surface. Will not be
surprised if future runs drop the snow level closer to 1000 feet.
Even with the low snow levels the ECMWF dissipates the shortwave
almost completely by 12z Friday. ECMWF ensemble solutions have
about half with precipitation, but very light. The GFS
operational run is much wetter than the ECMWF but looking at the
GFS ensembles the operational run is the outlier with ensemble
solutions that do indicate precipitation, once again about half,
only indicating light precipitation. Even in the Cascades at this
point only looking at a couple inches of new snow.
