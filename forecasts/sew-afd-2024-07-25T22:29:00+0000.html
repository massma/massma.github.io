.MARINE...
-- Changed Discussion --
High pressure over the northeastern Pacific will
interact with lower pressure inland east of the Cascades through
the weekend, promoting onshore flow and northwest winds in the
coastal waters. A small craft advisory remains in effect for the
central and eastern Strait of Juan de Fuca for westerly winds
15-20 kt with frequent gusts to 25 kt through late tonight. Winds
will then ease again during the overnight hours. The outer coastal
waters may see occasional northwesterly winds up to 20 kt, with
gusts to 25 kt Friday evening into Saturday. Additional pushes of
westerly winds also look likely down the Strait, so additional
rounds of headlines will be possible over the next few days. A
frontal system then looks to move into the area waters on Monday.

Wave heights over the coastal waters will generally range between
3 to 6 feet through the next several days. 14
