-- Changed Discussion --
An upper level trough and
onshore flow over the Pac NW will keep western WA near July
averages through the week. High temperatures will be in the 60s

.MARINE...
-- Changed Discussion --
Surface high pressure will remain offshore over the
eastern Pacific Ocean with low pressure inland, maintaining
northwesterly onshore flow.

Latest high-resolution ensemble guidance suggests around a 40-50%
chance of Small Craft Advisory winds early tonight through the
Central and Eastern Strait of Juan de Fuca. Frequent gusts up to 25
to 30 kt are possible, therefore will put a short-fused Small Craft
Advisory for these regions for tonight.

A stronger push is expected tomorrow evening with more solidly Small
Craft Advisory conditions through the Central and Eastern Strait, as
well as into Admiralty Inlet. Gusts up to near gale force are
possible, especially through the East Strait late in the day
Wednesday (50-60% probability), which has prompted a Gale Watch
Winds may also be breezy through the southern Strait of Georgia, but
not expecting anything reaching SCA.

Additional pushes on onshore winds are expected through the next few
days, which could yield additional headlines through the Strait of
Juan de Fuca at times.

Coastal seas 3 to 5 feet will generally remain through the rest of
the week.
