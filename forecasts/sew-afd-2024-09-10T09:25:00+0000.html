.SHORT TERM /TODAY THROUGH THURSDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Today, expect onshore flow,
morning stratus clouds and cooler conditions ahead of the next
system that will bring rain late this evening into Wednesday.
Morning marine stratus clouds are likely to linger through the
middle of the afternoon today. This next weather system will move
in from the Gulf of Alaska, keeping the temperatures only in the
upper 60s to low 70s.

The core of the upper level low will move through on Wednesday,
keeping showers in the forecast throughout the day. The period
with the highest likelihood for wetting rains will be during the
day on Wednesday. Storm total QPF from late Tuesday night through
Thursday morning shows around a quarter to half inch along the
coast, with around a quarter inch within Puget Sound. The forecast
for a few inches of snow at the higher elevations of Mount Rainier
is still on track.

Brief and weak shortwave ridging will build in the wake of the low
pressure system for Thursday, confining shower chances to the
mountains but increasing the temperatures slightly back into the
upper 60s and low 70s.

.LONG TERM /FRIDAY THROUGH MONDAY/...Models are suggestive of
another trough moving into the region during the later part of the
week, but less is known about how strong or amplified it may be.
Regardless, a cooler pattern with offshore troughing will promote
cooler temperatures and rain chances into the weekend. Both the 6
to 10 day and 8 to 14 day outlook from the Climate Prediction
Center favor below normal temperatures and above normal
precipitation chances for Washington.

Kristell

.AVIATION...
-- Changed Discussion --
Southwest flow aloft will continue over Western
Washington today as an upper trough digs southward offshore. The low
level flow is light onshore. Low level moisture and weak surface
flow will lead to a mixed bag of ceilings over Western Washington
this morning ranging from MVFR down to localized LIFR. A broad area
of MVFR ceilings in stratus expanded across central Puget Sound
overnight, but other areas remained clear longer and this will lead
to areas of IFR/LIFR ceilings with surface visibilities below
3SM...particularly across south Puget Sound and the Southwest
Interior. Ceilings will lift to VFR most areas by 18Z-20Z as mid and
high level moisture increases ahead of an approaching frontal
system. Ceilings will deteriorate back to MVFR in light rain along
the coast by this evening with said conditions spreading to the
interior lowlands overnight tonight.

KSEA...Somewhat messy scenario this morning as stratus deck around
2000-2500 feet expands southward across central Puget Sound. This
may either delay or eliminate the formation of any LIFR/IFR ceilings
in the immediate vicinity of the terminal...therefore confidence in
anything below MVFR is low. Ceilings will lift back to VFR this
afternoon as mid/high clouds thicken and lower ahead of incoming
system. Ceilings are expected to lower to low MVFR again by 09Z-12Z
Wednesday with some light rain spreading into the area. Surface
winds light southerly early becoming southwest 5 to 8 knots late
this morning onward. 27

.MARINE...
-- Changed Discussion --
Weak onshore flow will turn offshore tonight as a
weak surface low and associated frontal system shifts into the
coastal waters. Winds may briefly touch Small Craft Advisory levels
over portions of the outer coastal waters tonight, but the
vertically stacked nature of this system favors sub-advisory winds
as it reaches the waters. Broad surface ridging rebuilds offshore
for the second half of week for light onshore flow. Another weak
trough is slated to drop into the waters this weekend, but currently
appears to be of little consequence.   27
