.SHORT TERM /TODAY THROUGH THURSDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Latest NWS radar imagery
depicts some scattered showers pivoting through the region early
this morning in between a low off the southern Oregon coast and a
shortwave dropping south along coastal British Columbia.
Otherwise, relatively light rinds and abundant low level moisture
is supporting low stratus and some areas of fog. Expect these low
clouds to continue through around mid-morning with some continued
showers around the region. However, the transition to high
pressure and a less active weather pattern is then expected for
the next several days as the aforementioned low off the Oregon
coast pushes away from the region. Expect increasingly dry
conditions Wednesday and into the Thanksgiving holiday, but it`s
worth noting that a few weak impulses rounding the ridge may still
support some light showers at times. As a result, have maintained
the low PoPs evident in the NBM guidance, but the associated QPF
remains quite light. That is, even if there are a few showers
around at times, these are likely to have little accumulation.
With the building high pressure, expect to see overnight lows
gradually trending cooler by Thursday.

.LONG TERM /FRIDAY THROUGH MONDAY/...Looking ahead to the holiday
weekend, ensemble guidance continues to suggest that the dominant
feature will remain a relatively high amplitude across the far
eastern Pacific and extending into the Pacific Northwest. Again,
there is a non-trivial subset of ensemble members that suggest a
few passing disturbances in the flow could round the ridge and at
least bring a few showers to portions of the area. As mentioned in
the short term section, the associated precipitation amounts are
relatively light so while there is some mention of showers through
the weekend, it`s likely that there won`t be a significant round
of precipitation and only perhaps a few light showers at times
into the weekend. Meanwhile, with a shift to a cooler and somewhat
drier flow aloft, expect to see the air mass to cool a bit late
in the week and this weekend. This could bring overnight
temperatures down into the 30s (lower 30s in the coolest spots,
mid 30s for most of the Puget Sound Region) for a few mornings
during this time period. For the start of next week, still strong
agreement in the ensemble guidance suggesting a ridge remaining in
place, though increasing disagreement in just how strong it is
and where the ridge axis remains. 12
-- End Changed Discussion --
