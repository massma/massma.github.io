An upper level low is beginning to move into western Washington this
afternoon, with increasing shower activity into the evening. Recent
radar imagery depicts these scattered showers across the interior,
with most of the activity located within the South Sound.
Additionally, the air mass continues to be unstable this afternoon,
with a chance of thunderstorms possible into early this evening.
Latest hi- resolution guidance highlights most of the possible
thunderstorm activity to likely be in the SW interior, although
cannot rule out a thunderstorm over Puget Sound. The primary impacts
from any thunderstorms that do develop will be heavy downpours,
lightning, and some small hail or graupel. Snow levels will drop to
around 5000 ft with a few inches of snow possible on the higher
peaks in the mountains.

The upper level low will continue to move through western
Washington into Saturday, with the bulk of the shower activity
falling tonight into Saturday morning. Shower activity will
gradually decrease through Saturday, although a few showers will
likely linger in the mountains. High temperatures will remain in
the mid 60s for the interior.

The upper level low will move eastward on Sunday, allowing for
conditions to dry out across the area and warm up slightly, with
temperatures in the upper 60s and low 70s across the interior.
Conditions will continue to dry out as a broad high pressure
builds on Monday across the region, with temperatures warming into
the mid to upper 70s across the interior, with areas along the
water in the mid 60s.

.LONG TERM /TUESDAY THROUGH FRIDAY/...High pressure will remain
over the area over midweek, with temperatures in the interior
warming into the low to mid 80s, likely being the warmest day in
this stretch. Uncertainty lies going into the end of the week,
where ensembles are hinting at cooler but drier conditions, but
also a chance of some weak systems crossing the area. For now,
have kept mostly dry conditions resuming throughout the long term
period with high temperatures in the upper 60s into lower 70s.
