.SHORT TERM /TODAY THROUGH THURSDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
A vertically stacked
trough will move onshore over Oregon later this morning. A broad
upper level trough will continue to influence Western Washington
into Wednesday as little disturbances embedded in the trough kick
off a few showers from time to time. In addition, low level
onshore flow will lead to some convergence over Puget Sound
extending into the Cascades through tonight. Guidance suggests 1
to 4 inches of snow will fall in the Cascade passes by late this
evening.

The next weak disturbance will dip southward from British Columbia
on Wednesday keeping showers in the forecast for a broader portion
of the area and temperatures on the cool side. Then changes begin
to take place Wednesday night as drier northerly flow aloft
develops and the upper trough axis shifts east of the Cascades.
Upper ridging will start to build into the region on Thursday with
northerly flow at the surface developing at the surface by
Thursday afternoon. This should help scour out any remaining low
level moisture for some sunshine for the lowlands by mid-
afternoon. High temperatures will nudge upward slightly to near or
a little above normal.

.LONG TERM /FRIDAY THROUGH MONDAY/...The upper level ridge
continues to build into Western Washington on Friday with 500
millibar heights rising above 570 dam and the low level flow
turning weakly offshore. With plenty of sunshine, this will boost
much of the lowlands (including coastal areas) into the 60s.
The ridge axis moves onshore on Saturday for further warming into
the mid 60s to lower 70s for the lowlands. Some uncertainty
creeps into the forecast Sunday into Monday as ensembles disagree
on how quickly the ridge will weaken and flatten as it shifts
eastward. In general, the GFS ensembles are 12 to 24 hours faster
than the Euro ensembles with breaking the ridge down. The current
forecast is a compromise of the two with some increase in cloud
cover by late Sunday and a low end chance of rain. It`s worth
mentioning that ensembles generally agree on keeping a mean ridge
position over the interior West heading into early next week...so
it wouldn`t be surprising to see the front currently projected
for Sunday night and Monday lose some gusto in subsequent model
runs. 27

.MARINE...
-- Changed Discussion --
Onshore flow will increase today in the wake of a passing
surface low to the south. Small craft westerlies through the Strait
of Juan de Fuca are possible tonight, with the winds focused moreso
in the Central and Eastern Strait. Additionally, the outer coastal
waters, namely the northern zones, may see small craft winds as
well tonight.

Surface high pressure will then build midweek and into the
weekend for more benign conditions.

Coastal seas are hovering between 7 and 9 feet early this morning,
and are expected to decrease to around 6 feet by this afternoon. A
more notable decrease in sea state to around 4 feet will occur by
Thursday as high pressure begins to return to the region.
