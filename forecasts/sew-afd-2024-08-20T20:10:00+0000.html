.SHORT TERM /TONIGHT THROUGH SATURDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Shower activity
continues to track eastward across western Washington this
afternoon with individual bands/cells propagating to the
northeast. Thus far, only a couple of lightning strikes have been
noted over the coastal waters, with no strikes over land. That
said, there is enough instability around to support at least brief
thunderstorm development this afternoon. Sounding profiles and
mesoanalysis show very skinny CAPE, not supportive of much more
than brief heavy rain, very small hail, and erratic winds, and the
occasional lightning strike.

The upper level low driving this activity will move east of the
area overnight. Quick on its heels, another low pressure system
will dig south from the BC coast down to the coast of southern
Oregon Wednesday and Thursday, supporting yet more shower and
isolated thunder potential. The low is then expected to lift
northeast Friday into Saturday, with wrap-around showers
continuing through this period. In all, it`s unlikely that there
will be any period that will be completely dry across western
Washington between now and Saturday.

.LONG TERM /SUNDAY THROUGH TUESDAY/...By Sunday through the first
part of next week, confidence in the details falls off. Consensus
indicates roughly zonal flow, keeping temperatures cool, but
resulting in longer breaks between shower activity. Looking out a
bit further, long range outlooks show the potential for ridging to
develop leading to dry conditions and temperatures rising to near
or above normal once again.
-Wolcott-
-- End Changed Discussion --
