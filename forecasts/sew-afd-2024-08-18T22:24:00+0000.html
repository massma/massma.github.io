.SYNOPSIS...

A strong upper level low will continue to spin
offshore through today, continuing to provide an unstable

.SHORT TERM /TONIGHT THROUGH WEDNESDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Recent radar imagery
shows a few convective showers moving through Grays Harbor
county into Mason County, moving northward. There are also some
thunderstorms developing in southern Lewis County also moving
northeastward. This will largely be in the trend through the
remainder of the afternoon, with showers and possible
thunderstorms developing south of our area (Lewis, Grays Harbor
county) moving north, towards the Olympic Peninsula and areas
adjacent. In any thunderstorm that does develop, the primary
impacts are gusty erratic winds, frequent lightning, and heavy
rain. Again, coverage from these storms will be less than what we
saw yesterday, mostly confined to the western portion of our CWA,
but cannot rule out a stray storms drifting into Puget Sound this
evening.

This activity will likely last through the early evening hours.
As we progress into the nighttime hours, mostly all activity will
diminish as diurnal heating gets shut off and instability values
plummet.

The upper level low offshore will continue to move northeast
offshore into Tuesday. As of now, dry conditions will start of
Monday with perhaps areas of clouds and fog, with temperatures in
the mid 70s. Chance of rain showers will uptick across the area
Monday night into Tuesday, most likely having to do with wrap
around moisture from the aforementioned upper level low.

Another upper level low will drop down from British Columbia on
Wednesday night, which could provide another round of showers and
scattered thunderstorms across the area. he track of this upper
low is still uncertain, along with the placement. But for now, the
highest probabilities of thunderstorms seem to be located in the
Olympics and the Cascades, with the potential (20-25%) for the
interior lowlands.

.LONG TERM /THURSDAY THROUGH SUNDAY/...Ensembles continue to
highlight unsettled weather throughout the long term period. The
aforementioned upper level low will continue to track southward
through Friday, with a chance of showers/thunderstorms, primarily
Thursday and Friday. The ensembles are starting to show signals of
possibly a ridge building into Sunday, which could bring back the
warm and dry conditions to our region.

Mazurkiewicz

.FIRE WEATHER...
-- Changed Discussion --
An upper level low will continue to remain in the
region into Monday. A disturbance has already triggered scattered
thunderstorms this afternoon from the Southwest Interior into the
Olympics. There is also a chance of thunderstorms in the North
Cascades later this evening. Rainfall amounts will be
significantly lower across the area, with the best chance of
wetting rain in the Olympics. With continued cool, cloudy, and
high humidity conditions well into the week, holdover fires may
only smolder for quite sometime until we get our next hot, dry, or
windy conditions.

Mazurkiewicz
