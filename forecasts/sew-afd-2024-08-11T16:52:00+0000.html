.AVIATION...
-- Changed Discussion --
Northwest flow aloft with upper level troughing over
the region. Marine stratus has filled in over most of western
Washington. Ceilings under the stratus are ranging between MVFR to
LIFR, as well as some visibility restrictions at times. Stratus will
be slow to retreat this this afternoon for the interior with a
gradual return to VFR conditions around 18-21z. The coast, however,
does not look to clear out today and will remain under low ceilings
through the TAF period. Another widespread push of marine stratus is
likely to redevelop overnight into Monday morning. Winds will remain
west-southwesterly 5 to 10 kt throughout the TAF period.

KSEA...MVFR to IFR continue this morning at the terminal with
occasional periods of limited visibility in mist/fog. Ceilings will
likely remain in the MVFR category until clouds start to scatter out
around 20-22Z. Skies should be mostly clear and return to VFR by 23Z
this afternoon. VFR conditions remain through the evening until
marine stratus and lower ceilings return once again overnight.Winds

.MARINE...
-- Changed Discussion --
A broad 1032mb NE Pacific surface high is well offshore
but is maintaining onshore flow here across area waters. Steep seas
across will continue today through the outer coastal waters with
waves 6 to 8 ft at 8 seconds. A Small Craft Advisory remains in
effect for the outer coastal waters north of Point Grenville through
this afternoon. Seas subside Monday back to 3 to 5 ft and remain
like so through the week.

.FIRE WEATHER...
-- Changed Discussion --
Elevated fire weather conditions will persist
this afternoon and evening across portions of the North Cascades
as another shortwave trough drops into the area and brings the
potential for thunderstorm development. Coverage across the
western slopes looks to be less than that of the previous days,
however any thunderstorms that do develop may produce frequent
lightning, brief heavy downpours, hail, and gusty and erratic
winds. Thunderstorms will be possible across the North Cascades
again Monday afternoon and evening again, however probabilities
wane as the greater threat moves off to the east.

Broader troughing setting up over the region this week will
promote increased onshore flow and good relative humidities across
the region. This will then help alleviate fire weather conditions
through at least midweek. 14
