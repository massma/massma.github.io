.SHORT TERM /TODAY THROUGH FRIDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Satellite imagery shows
Monday`s front weakening along the Oregon border. Partly to mostly
cloudy skies in the wake of the system over Western Washington.
Doppler radar not picking up any shower activity at 3 am/10z.
Temperatures were in the mid 40s to mid 50s.

Pretty quiet weather today across Western Washington. Cloud cover
decreasing a little over the southern portion of the area while a
weak shortwave moving by to the north will keep a slight chance of
showers near the Canadian border this afternoon. Weak upper level
trough over Western Washington this morning will dissipate this
afternoon. Cooler air aloft from the trough will keep high
temperatures in the upper 50s to mid 60s, slightly below normal.

Upper level ridge building tonight with light surface gradients.
The combination of light flow in the lower levels and longer
nights this time of year will create some fog in the fog prone
areas like the Southwest Interior and interior river valleys early
Thursday morning. Lack of cloud cover will produce some cool low
temperatures with some locations in the Southwest Interior
dropping into the upper 30s. Lows in the 40s for the remainder of
the area.

Upper level ridge shifting east Thursday but next front still well
to the west making for a sunny day. The fog that does form will be
shallow and dissipate by late morning. Highs temperatures a few
degrees warmer than today, in the 60s.

Next front approaching the area Thursday night. Rain out ahead of
the front reaching the North Coast in the early morning hours.
Chance of rain for the rest of the area west of Puget Sound after
midnight. Increasing clouds will prevent the temperatures from
dropping as much as Thursday morning. Lows in the 40s.

Front moving through Western Washington later Friday. Rain out
ahead of the front moving into the area Friday morning. Parent low
associated with the front weakening as it moves into Central
British Columbia during the day. Even with the low well to the
north and weakening some gusty southerly winds over the area just
ahead and with the front Friday afternoon. Rain most of the day.
Highs near 60.

.AVIATION...
-- Changed Discussion --
Zonal west flow aloft will continue, with northerly
offshore low level flow. Post frontal clouds are still lingering in
and north of Puget Sound. These have mixed with bases at 2,200 feet
up to 3,500 feet. Combined with this morning`s marine push post-
front, will most likely see lower-end VFR clouds over most terminals
this morning (given lingering moisture that will dry out throughout
the day). With relatively good cloud cover, threat for mist/fog at a
few terminals will decrease, but cannot rule out some patches of fog
(especially towards the Cascades). Skies will clear later today.
Most terminals will see light south winds this morning below 5 kt
(exceptions being near the coastline and Strait of Juan de Fuca with
westerlies beginning to turn northerly), with winds become
northerly/northeasterly everywhere Wednesday afternoon/evening.

KSEA...CIGs hover this morning in between VFR/MVFR (with the
dominant base being around 6,000 feet and low clouds cycling
coverages at 2,000-2,500 feet). These may linger through as late as
18Z before clearing. Northeast winds continue around 8 kt this
morning. Guidance shows winds weakening to southerlies under 5 kt
after 12Z, before picking back up from the north/northeast at 5 kt
later this afternoon/evening.

.MARINE...
-- Changed Discussion --
Continue to observe hazardous seas behind a weak cold
front that went through the waters yesterday. Have observed wave
heights of 11 to 13 feet at 12 to 14 seconds at buoy 41 this
morning. With the current west/northwest swell, these waves are
expected to continue through overnight Wednesday/early Thursday
morning. Winds also continue to be gusty in the central/east Strait
of Juan de Fuca, and will decrease later this morning below small
craft advisory thresholds. Headlines/products remain unchanged for
this update.

Expect the flow to become more northerly Thursday as ridging builds
back in southern British Columbia. The next frontal system will pass
through Thursday/Friday. It appears at this time that there may be
gusty winds through the Strait of Juan de Fuca. Otherwise
predictability of hazards still remains relatively low at this time.

Seas will drop gradually down to 4 to 6 feet Thursday/Friday,
building back to 5 to 7 feet towards the end of the week.
