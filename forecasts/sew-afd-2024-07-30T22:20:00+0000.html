.SHORT TERM /TONIGHT THROUGH FRIDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Onshore flow throughout the
morning has maintained cloudy skies across western Washington,
with light showers and drizzle lingering over higher terrain
mainly north of the Puget Sound. Cloud cover will persist well
into the afternoon with only partial clearing, which will limit
temperatures across the region several degrees below normal in the
low 70s.

Dry conditions will return on Wednesday as zonal flow develops
aloft and high pressure develops offshore. Cloudy conditions will
scour out by the morning, allowing for temperatures to return to
near normal, near 70 degrees along the coast and the upper 70s to
lower 80s further inland.

High pressure will continue to build northward into western
Washington through the remainder of the week as a strong ridge
amplifies over the western US. Much of the region will see a
return of elevated temperatures paired with dry conditions, with
the lowlands east of the Puget Sound on track to reach 90 degrees
by Friday afternoon. Friday will be the hottest day of the period,
with moderate (orange) HeatRisk potential across many lowlands
surrounding the Puget Sound. The immediate coast will remain much
cooler in the 70s with a persistent belt of marine stratus
expected to cool things off under weak onshore flow.

.LONG TERM /SATURDAY THROUGH TUESDAY/...Ensembles remain in good
agreement over hot and dry conditions through the weekend and
into early next week. Weak troughing offshore is likely to cool
things off a few degrees over the weekend, with an influx of cloud
cover likely on Saturday. Low-level onshore flow will also likely
limit high tempeartures over the weekend and into next week,
though above normal temperatures will continue. Forecast models
continue to show the potential for thunderstorms over higher
terrain triggered by weak shortwaves moving across the region, but
confidence is low at this time.

Lindeman
