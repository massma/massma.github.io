.SHORT TERM /TODAY THROUGH WEDNESDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
No significant updates
this morning to the forecast. Refer to the previous discussion
below and updated aviation section.

Latest satellite imagery shows clouds continuing to scatter
across the interior, while overcast conditions persist across the
coast and Strait of Juan de Fuca this morning. We should be able
to get some more scattering of the low level clouds this
afternoon, but high clouds will also begin to move in tonight as a
weak upper- level shortwave moves through. Highs today should be
warmer than yesterday, in the low to mid 70s. Another weak
shortwave will move through Tuesday night, keeping skies mostly
cloudy and reinforcing onshore flow. As such, temperatures Tuesday
will only be a few degrees warmer than today, in the upper 70s,
with some low 80s possible in the Cascade valleys. On Wednesday, a
larger and more moist upper level trough will move through the
area, which will introduce an associated frontal system that will
move through Wednesday. PoPs increase, with the best chances for
seeing light rain along the coast and the northern Cascades.
Temperatures will also cool off, with highs in the upper 60s.

.AVIATION...
-- Changed Discussion --
Weak upper ridging will bring light westerly flow
aloft through today. It`s mainly a mix of VFR/MVFR cigs across
the area terminals this morning. Satellite shows stratus has
made it into the Southwest Interior and portions of the Sound
all the way to the Northern Cascades this morning. The only
areas remaining clear this morning are portions of the central
and south Sound. Though, the latest satellite imagery is already
indicating stratus slowly retreating across the interior. Cigs
across the interior will improve back to VFR midday through the
afternoon with high clouds streaming overhead at times. MVFR
cigs may continue for the immediate coast through this
afternoon. Light southerly winds this morning will transition to
more W/NW this afternoon.

KSEA...VFR conditions. Latest satellite imagery shows stratus
staying out of the terminal. Predominantly light SW winds this
morning, transitioning to NW this afternoon (between 22z-00z).
Winds will become more northerly tonight. JD/SB

.MARINE...
-- Changed Discussion --
Surface high pressure will build over the waters
through today. Generally light winds are expected today with
weak onshore flow. Onshore flow will increase late Tuesday,
which may bring Small Craft Advisory west winds through the

70% for gusts of 25 kts late Tuesday for this region. Onshore
flow will then continue through late this week. Northerly winds

pressure building over the area.

Seas of 3 to 5 feet will continue through the week. JD
