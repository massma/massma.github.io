.SHORT TERM /TONIGHT THROUGH FRIDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Low pressure has moved
inland today with onshore flow increasing across western WA. The
result is more clouds and cooler temps across the region with
highs (so far) in the upper 40s to lower 50s. Winds have been
gusting through the strait and there`s a convergence zone forming
over Snohomish County this afternoon. The convergence zone will
sway between King and Snohomish tonight with showers mainly
impacting the central Cascades. Snow level will hover around 3500
ft with a rain/snow mix at Snoqualmie Pass and light snow (up to
3") at Stevens.

A broad trough over the West will keep the forecast cool and
showery moving into Wednesday. Morning lows will be chilly and in
the mid 30s around the south sound, with low 40s elsewhere. Expect
highs around 50 for most spots. Snow levels remain low (around
2500-3000 ft) with a few more inches of snow in the mountains and
passes.

We`re under drier, N flow, on Thursday as high pressure builds
offshore. Weak ripples in the flow will keep a chance of showers
in the forecast, particularly in the Cascades, although total
amounts will be light. Morning lows will be chilly again and in
the 30s for most locations. Highs will be a little warmer and in
the mid 50s.

The ridge will nudge inland on Friday for dry and warmer
conditions. Expect highs in the 60s with plenty of sun. 33

.LONG TERM /SATURDAY THROUGH TUESDAY/...High pressure keeps the
region warm and dry moving into the weekend. Temperatures will
reach the 60s to lower 70s both Saturday and Sunday. Rain returns
late Sunday night and Monday as high pressure shifts farther
inland and a weak front moves in from the west. A trough brings
more wet and locally windy weather on Tuesday with overall cooler
conditions. 33
