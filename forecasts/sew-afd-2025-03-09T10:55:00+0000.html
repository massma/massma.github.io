.SHORT TERM /TODAY THROUGH TUESDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Satellite imagery shows
weakening atmospheric river over Southern British Columbia with
the trailing cold front approaching 130W early this morning. With
the river this far north only Forks reporting rain at 3 am/10z
with some breaks in the cloud cover from Seattle southward.
Wide range of temperatures with the breaks in the cloud cover
allowing the South Puget Sound and the Southwest Interior to drop
into the upper 30s while cloudy skies keeping temperatures near 50
in Whatcom county.

Not much change in the pattern early this morning with a cool
upper level trough digging south near 135W preventing much
eastward movement of the river and cold front. Trough shifting
east later this morning into this afternoon pushing what is left
of the atmospheric river back down over Western Washington with
rain spreading over the area from north to south. Windy
conditions around the eastern entrance of the Strait of Juan de
Fuca ahead of the cold front with gusts to 50 mph possible on
Whidbey Island and near Admiralty Inlet. A wind advisory has been
issued for this location. Highs in the upper 40s to mid 50s.

Cold front moving through Western Washington this evening and into
the Cascades by early Monday morning bringing an end to the
steady precipitation. Post frontal convergence zone developing
quickly over Snohomish and northern King county. Snow levels
lowering to 3500 feet. Up to a foot of new snow possible in the
Northern and Central Cascades with the highest amounts expected
around Mount Baker and in the convergence zone around Stevens
Pass. Outside of the convergence zone and the mountains post
frontal shower activity pretty minimal. With some breaks in the
cloud cover temperatures dropping to near freezing along the Hood
Canal and in the Southwest Interior with mid to upper 30s
elsewhere.

Not much going on Monday. Weak onshore flow with skies mostly
cloudy. Air mass slightly unstable with the cool air mass overhead
keeping a chance of showers in the forecast. Highs will be below
normal, near 50.

Another weather system approaching the north coast Monday night
but once again a cold trough digging south along 140W will stop
the eastward progress of the front leaving it west of the area
early Tuesday morning. Front starts to move eastward again Tuesday
afternoon with the front reaching the coast Tuesday night. Rain
out ahead of the system spreading over the area Tuesday afternoon
and evening. Lows in the mid 30s to near 40 with highs near 50.

.MARINE...
-- Changed Discussion --
A stalled front offshore will continue to bring elevated
southerly winds over the coastal waters through this morning. Winds
will mostly be in the Small Craft Advisory range through a few gusts
to gale force will remain possible this morning. Breezy winds will
continue to persist into the early afternoon for the Northern Inland
Waters, the East Entrance to the Strait of Juan de Fuca, and
Admiralty Inlet, where a gale warning remains in effect. Puget Sound
will see Small Craft Advisory strength winds. Winds will rapidly
switch to westerly behind the frontal passage. A substantial push of
westerlies down the Central and Eastern Strait of Juan de Fuca looks
to follow the front late this afternoon into early this evening.

High pressure rebuilds across the area tonight into Monday but
another frontal system looks to move towards the coastal waters and
stall just offshore on Tuesday. This may allow for a potentially
stronger low pressure system to move towards the area waters Tuesday
night into Wednesday, bringing another round of breezy winds and
elevated seas. The active pattern looks to continue into late week.

Seas 10 to 13 ft will slowly decrease to around 8 ft tomorrow.
Another system Tuesday into Wednesday looks to raise seas back to
around 10 to 13 ft and remaining around or above 10 ft through the
rest of the week.
