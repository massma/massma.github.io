.MARINE...
-- Changed Discussion --
Broad surface high pressure over the Pacific Northwest
is lending itself to tranquil conditions over coastal waters
today. A weak frontal system will move through Saturday morning.
Then, a strong 1036 mb high pressure system will quickly usher in
behind the front. This will allow for breezy northwesterly winds
to develop, beginning Saturday afternoon. A thermal surface trough
attempts to develop along the coast, but looks to be mostly
confined to southern Oregon into California, where the strongest
winds will be along the West Coast. These winds will persist all
the way through at least Tuesday as the high slowly retreats
westward. Wind speeds look to remain in the Small Craft Advisory
category, but gusts to low-end gale force cannot be ruled out
during this period.

As for the interior waters, a westerly push down the Strait of
Juan de Fuca is expected Saturday afternoon/evening behind the
frontal system which looks to reach Small Craft Advisory criteria.
Following, west/northwest winds will prevail into early next
week with no additional headlines expected at this time.

Seas remain 4 to 6 ft today. Seas begin to increase Saturday
afternoon up to 8 to 12 ft and will remain there through Tuesday.
Waves may reach as high as 12-14 ft in the outer coastal waters
during the periods of strongest winds. The dominant period will be
quite short, around 7 to 8 seconds, making for very choppy seas
Saturday evening through Tuesday.
