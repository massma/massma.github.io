-- Changed Discussion --
Isolated to scattered showers are spread across the
region this morning. A weak and narrow convergence band was
moving north across Island and Snohomish counties at the time of
writing, and popcorn showers were moving ENE onshore across the
Coast and Olympic Peninsula. These showers are associated with a
trough axis that will move across the area today. By mid-day, some
minor daytime heating will result in 100-250 J/kg SBCAPE and
allow for some weak thunderstorm activity. The main threats with
any storms will be brief heavy downpours, small hail, and
lightning. Weak convection could continue overnight before
tapering off Friday. The current forecast is on track but will be
monitoring for any needed changes.

.SHORT TERM /TODAY THROUGH SATURDAY/...Radar imagery this morning
shows scattered showers across the interior, with snow in the
mountains. Snow levels this morning generally in the 1000 to 1500
feet, with the Cascades and passes receiving accumulating snow
(possibly heavy at times) early this morning as a batch of heavier
moisture persists over the mountains. A Winter Weather Advisory
continues until noon. Scattered showers will continue throughout
the day over the interior as an upper trough axis shifts
overhead. The air mass will be unstable this afternoon which may
lead to the chance of an isolated thunderstorm or two, especially
this evening. The greatest areas of instability values look to be
over SW washington, but regardless, a few isolated lightning
strikes can be possible over the interior this afternoon.

Cooler and wetter weather is on track for Friday as another
shortwave system pushes through the region, with most of the
energy associated with this located more southward. Another day
of rain showers is to be expected, with snowfall in the mountains.
Snow levels look to raise slightly to around 2000 to 2500 feet by
late Friday night.

The active weather pattern continues, with a steadier stream of
moisture aimed at western WA starting Friday night into Saturday,
with another round of lowland rain and with westerly orographic
flow, could support more significant snowfall in the mountains as
well. Could also see some breezy winds across the area with the
a surface low looming offshore, mainly for areas near the
coastline.

.LONG TERM /SUNDAY THROUGH WEDNESDAY/...Additional systems look to
impact the area continuing through the weekend, with more lowland
rain, mountain snow, and breezy conditions. Snow will continue
through the weekend with some locations in the Cascades receiving
potentially 2 feet of fresh snow, with snow levels generally
hovering around 2000-2500 feet. Heavier precipitation in the
Olympics may drive the Skokomish River in Mason County into flood
stage. Conditions look to lighten up through midweek, with
additional showers likely each day before a ridge attempt to set
up at the end of the long term period.

Mazurkiewicz

.MARINE...
-- Changed Discussion --
Small craft advisory remains in effect for the coastal
waters this morning as seas are holding in the 9 to 11 ft range, and
the SCA has been extended into the evening hours as waves hold on.
Otherwise,  another round of gusty southerly winds again tonight
into early Friday. As additional disturbances continue to push
through the region (one Friday, another around Sunday), expect to
see both winds and seas increase and additional advisories will be

next week peak around around 40% over the coastal waters Friday and

to issue a gale watch at this time. However, seas around 15 ft are
expected over the coastal waters at times Friday and into the
weekend, and likely remain above 10 ft through the early part of
next week.
