.AVIATION...
-- Changed Discussion --
Westerly flow aloft early this evening will become more
north to northwesterly as upper level trough axis moves through W
WA. Surface winds have already started to turn south to
southwesterly, with remaining terminals following suit by late this
evening and becoming more solidly southwesterly by late Sunday
morning. Most terminals seeing wind speeds around 3 to 6 kts and
will remain that way for the overnight period before increasing to 5
to 10 kts by noon Sunday. HQM running a little faster with 5 to 10
kts expected throughout the TAF period.

VFR conditions remain in place for majority of area this evening
with IFR to LIFR conditions present along the coast due to marine
stratus. This stratus will work its way inland thanks to a strong
onshore push tonight. How far inland is the major forecast problem
as some model data suggesting that it may not make it any further
west than the western banks of the Sound. Current forecast actually
has it getting to the east side...impacting SEA and BFI. May pull
that back a bit in 06Z issuance...still allowing for the prospect
of lower cigs, but relegating them to a TEMPO group given current
models latest lack of confidence. Haze this evening should give way
as onshore flow becomes more prevalent and should not be an issue
for Sunday.

KSEA...VFR conditions through most of the TAF period. As mentioned
above, models backing away from MVFR/IFR conditions overnight, but
will leave the prospect for them as a TEMPO group. West to
northwesterly winds this evening before turning southwesterly early
Sunday morning. Speeds this evening and overnight ranging 3-6 kts
before picking up a little bit to 5-10 kts by noon Sunday.

.MARINE...
-- Changed Discussion --
High pressure over the East Pacific with lower pressure
over land will allow for northwesterly onshore flow to continue with
diurnally driven pushes of westerly winds down the Strait of Juan de
Fuca. High resolution guidance is indicating that the push this
afternoon should largely remain under Small Craft Advisory
threshold, through there exists a 30-40% chance for some occasional
SCA gusts this evening. Latest observations confirm this...but with
only gusts meeting SCA criteria, will hold off on any headlines at
this time...but will continue to monitor.

Seas currently 4 ft at 7 seconds before increasing Saturday evening
and into Sunday as winds increase over the coastal waters, reaching
6 to 8 ft. With the dominant period being around 8 seconds, seas
will be steep and look to approach steepness criteria for a SCA.
Seas will subside back to 3 to 5 ft on Monday and into next week.

.FIRE WEATHER...An upper level shortwave trough will brush
Washington to the north today and make its way eastward over the
weekend. Thunderstorms have started to develop along the Cascade
crest this afternoon and expect thunderstorm activity to continue
through this evening. While guidance continues to suggest a 10-20%
chance of thunderstorms across the Olympics today, highest
probabilities for thunderstorm development remain across the
North Cascades at this time. Main threats with any thunderstorms
are lightning and gusty and erratic winds. Lightning strikes will
have the potential to create new fire starts.

A Red Flag Warning remains in effect for the North Cascades zone
658 for thunderstorms and gusty outflow winds through this
evening. Isolated thunderstorms have also developed this
afternoon across zone 659 and may continue to develop,
particularly near Mount Rainier, however, thunderstorm coverage
continues to look more isolated for this area.

Threats for thunderstorms will continue across portions of the
Cascades through the weekend, however chances will gradually wane
as the shortwave moves off to the east. 14
