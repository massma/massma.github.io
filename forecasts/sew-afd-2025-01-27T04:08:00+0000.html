.SYNOPSIS...An upper level ridge centered offshore will gradually
build into the region through the early portion of the week. This
will lead to dry conditions and chilly overnight temperatures.
The ridge will weaken by midweek and a series of systems will
bring a return of lowland rain and mountain snow Friday into the
weekend, with a threat of lowland snow late in the weekend.

.SHORT TERM /TONIGHT THROUGH THURSDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
No major changes have
been made to the forecast this evening as things are still stable.
The rest of the previous discussion can be found below with an
update to the aviation and marine sections.

High pressure at the surface and aloft continues to provide dry,
cool conditions across the area with light low level offshore
flow. Little change in the overall pattern is expected heading
into Monday with more chilly nights ahead and sunny, cool days.
Surface gradients will be lighter and this will contribute to a
little more patchy freezing fog/low clouds across the Southwest
Interior and Puget Sound the next couple mornings. The upper ridge
begins to weaken and get suppressed southward Tuesday into
Wednesday as a system rides over the top of it into north/central
British Columbia. This will begin the process of opening the door
to a series of systems reaching Western Washington at the end of
the week.

.LONG TERM /FRIDAY THROUGH MONDAY/...Unsurprisingly, the models
are pushing back the arrival of the first front a smidge. Current
model timing generally agrees with bringing rain to the coast
late Thursday night and spreading precip across the interior
Friday morning. The front looks to stall a bit offshore before
getting shoved inland Friday night with rain and a solid shot of
mountain snow. Beyond this, the models crucially began to diverge
with respect to the depth and track of the trailing upper trough.

As discussed yesterday, this continues to have significant
implications in terms of both precip type and amounts moving into
the Saturday night and Sunday period. Taking a closer look at
both ensemble meteograms and NBM output, snow probabilities are
beginning to rise sharply by Sunday. The NBM now shows a 50-60%
probability of a fraction of an inch of snow by Sunday afternoon
over the entirety of lowland Western Washington. Those
probabilities fall to 25-35% for an inch. But given these numbers
7 days out, it`s becoming quite apparent that we`ll need to be
watching this evolving scenario closely in the days ahead. 27
