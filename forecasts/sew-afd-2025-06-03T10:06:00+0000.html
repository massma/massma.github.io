.SHORT TERM /TODAY THROUGH THURSDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Strong high pressure remains
anchored in the eastern Pacific through the next several days.
This will maintain generally dry and mild conditions across the
region through midweek, though a weak passing disturbance Wednesday
will likely clip the region and chance the onshore flow. This
will knock a few degrees off of the high temperatures, increase
the cloud cover across the region Wednesday morning, and
perhaps even bring some light drizzle along the coast.
Temperatures reach the lower 70s in the warmest spots Tuesday,
but a few degrees cooler Wednesday. With weaker onshore flow and
the ridge more squarely building overhead, Thursday is likely
the warmest of the three with afternoon readings peaking in the
upper 70s for most of the region.

.LONG TERM /THURSDAY NIGHT THROUGH MONDAY/...In a bit of a
repeat of Wednesday, Friday will likely feature another
shortwave disturbance passing across the region. This will
induce deeper onshore flow and bring more widespread clouds and
cooler afternoon temperatures. Ensemble guidance remains in
strong agreement with respect to the ridge building eastward
into the early portion of next week. This would bring afternoon
high temperatures increasing into the 80s for the start of next
week from around Seattle southward. This would correspond to
another round of moderate (Orange) HeatRisk values for next
weekend.
-- End Changed Discussion --

.MARINE...

A surface ridge will remain centered around 900
nautical miles offshore through much of the week ahead. Weak
