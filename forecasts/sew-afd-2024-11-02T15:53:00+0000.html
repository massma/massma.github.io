ensembles are not so quick to come to that conclusion, allowing
precip...although in pretty low amounts...to persist. Inherited
slight chance to chance PoPs during this time frame covers this well.

A stronger Pacific system makes its may to WA on Monday with
another round of rain and windy weather (mainly coast and north
interior), and heavy mountain snow (central and north Cascades).
Snow levels initially start low around 3500` but increase through
the day to around 4000-4500`. A strong onshore push develops
Monday night with the focus for wind impacts switching to the
strait and Admiralty Inlet area.

Temps in the near term remain pretty static, daytime highs in the
lower to mid 50s and overnight lows in the lower to mid 40s.

.MARINE...A surface trough continues to move inland through
Washington this morning. Saturday will allow for a period of
relatively benign conditions with the lull between systems. A cold
front will move through the waters tonight into Sunday.
Northwesterly winds behind the cold front look to increase to Small
Craft Advisory strength across the outer coastal zones. A push of
westerlies down the Strait of Juan de Fuca may create periods of
Small Craft Advisory strength winds through early Sunday morning, in
particular through the East Entrance and Admiralty Inlet. Winds will
ease during the day on Sunday before another system approaches the
area on Monday. Widespread gusty winds, potentially reaching gale
force, are possible ahead of the front. Strong westerly winds will
continue behind the front Monday afternoon into Monday evening,
where gale force winds are likely through the Strait of Juan de
Fuca. A few gusts approaching storm force cannot be ruled out during
this period through the Central and East Strait.

High pressure will build across the area Tuesday through mid-week,
allowing winds to ease on Tuesday and remain benign through the week.

Seas across the coastal waters remain around 7 to 9 ft and remain
like so through tonight. Seas will begin to rise above 10 ft
tonight. Long period swell being generated by a low pressure system
in the Gulf of Alaska is on track to arrive Sunday morning, bringing
waves 14 to 18 ft with a dominant period of around 19 seconds. This
may bring high surf conditions to the Pacific Coast. Seas will
rapidly rise Monday morning as the next low pressure system moves
through the area waters, with seas up to 18 to 22 feet possible
(with the highest seas being in the outer water zones). This will be
another period to monitor for high surf conditions. Seas will slowly
subside Monday night into Tuesday, below 10 ft by Tuesday night and
remain around 5 to 8 ft through mid-week.
