Previous discussion...A majority of low stratus surrounding the
Puget Sound has burned off this afternoon, but sun breaks in the
clouds will be short lived this afternoon as a storm system moves
into western Washington. A band of rainfall will continue to
progress inland this afternoon, overspreading the region by the
late evening. Conditions will continue to destabilize behind the
initial band of rainfall, with showers continuing throughout the
day Saturday. Model guidance continues to favor shower development
over the North Cascades on Saturday, as well as maintaining a 20%
to 30% chance of a few lightning strikes. A surface front will
slide across western Washington later in the day on Saturday,
shutting down most of the precipitation, with the exception of
Puget Sound convergence zone activity that will continue into
Saturday night. Rainfall totals will generally range between a
tenth and half an inch across the lowlands, with higher amounts up
to an inch over the mountains.

The upper level trough will shift southward on Sunday, with light
isolated showers continuing mainly south of the Puget Sound.
Cloudy skies and below-normal temperatures will continue with weak
high pressure slowly amplifying inland from the west.

.LONG TERM /WEDNESDAY THROUGH SATURDAY/...Ensembles continue to show
good agreement over split flow developing to start off next week,
allowing conditions to briefly dry out. Another storm system will
move into the Pacific Northwest from British Columbia on Tuesday,
bringing in another round of rainfall across western Washington
mid-week. Deterministic models are suggesting ridging developing
later next week, though ensembles hint at the potential for a
shortwave passage that may keep a chance of showers for the area.
Temperatures will stay below normal through the period, peaking in
the 60s across the lowlands.
