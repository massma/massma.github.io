.SHORT TERM /TONIGHT THROUGH SUNDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
A cold front is passing
through western WA this afternoon with post-frontal onshore flow
tonight (Puget Sound Convergence Zone setting up over the
King/Snohomish county line). The air mass is slightly unstable
with a chance of thunderstorms in the interior and Cascades (and
within the PSCZ tonight). Most areas seeing measurable rain with
this system with wetting rains in the Cascades. There will be
lingering showers with the convergence zone late tonight.

We`ll see a brief break on Thursday, then more rain and wind
Thursday night into Friday as the next Pacific frontal system
moves in. The coast and North Interior will be the windiest with
south gusts to 30-45 MPH. Total rainfall amounts are less compared
to today`s system with the focus mainly over the northern Olympic
peninsula and North Cascades. Showers will decrease toward the
afternoon as this system lifts north. Temperature-wise, we`ll
continue to track a few degrees cooler than average with highs in
the mid 60s and lows in the 50s.

There`s broad, weak troughing over the region moving into Saturday
but the there`s little moisture or forcing. Overall it`s looking
like a cool and cloudy forecast with dry conditions.  33

.LONG TERM /MONDAY THROUGH THURSDAY/...Weak ridging replaces the
trough on Sunday for more cool but dry weather. The wind flow
pattern does turn offshore for less cloud cover. Heights rise
slightly on Monday with a warmer air mass and warmer temps (closer
to average). Ensemble members are mostly in favor for rain
returning Tuesday and Wednesday as the ridge shifts east and
another trough moves in from the Pacific. 33
-- End Changed Discussion --

.MARINE...A cold front continues to move through the waters this
afternoon. Rain will continue to move across with this front, with
some of the stronger showers reducing visibilities at times (thunder
threat is low). Strong southerly winds continue to diminish behind
the front (buoy 41 showing gusts to 20 kt and seas dropping from
13.5 feet earlier this afternoon down to 6 to 8 feet). The gusty
wind threat continues for the central/east Strait of Juan de Fuca
(small craft advisory continues through late tonight with the threat
of westerlies of 15 to 25 kt with gusts to 35 kt possible).

A second front remains on track to pass through the region Thursday
afternoon into Friday. Widespread gusty southerly winds 20 to 30 kt
(with gusts to 35 kt) are expected over the coastal waters Thursday
afternoon and evening. Post front, gusty winds are expected to make
their way through the Strait of Juan de Fuca, and some of the inland
waters. In addition, seas will build back up to 13 to 16 feet at 10
to 12 seconds (with the highest seas in the outer coastal waters).
Behind the front, winds will turn to the west and northwest, and
remain only breezy in the outer coastal waters Friday and Saturday
(up to 20 kt). Seas will drop to 6 to 8 feet for the weekend, and
further decrease to 4 to 6 feet next week.
