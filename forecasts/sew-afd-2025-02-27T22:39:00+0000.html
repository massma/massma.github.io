.SHORT TERM /TONIGHT THROUGH SUNDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Satellite imagery shows
plentiful sun across the area with some thin high level cloud
cover. The remaining of the day will continue remain dry due to
upper level ridging over western Washington. High temperatures
over the area this afternoon will generally range from the upper
50s into the lower 60s.

These warm and dry conditions will continue into Friday as upper
level ridging remains over the area. Areas of some patchy fog are
possible early Friday morning, developing along the coast and in
the Chehalis valley. Another warm day is on tap, with afternoon
high temperatures continuing to be in the upper 50s into the low
60s for much for the interior.

The aforementioned upper level ridge axis will shift eastward on
Saturday, allowing for an upper level trough to enter the region.
Uncertainty still remains in the exact track of this trough, but
likely will see increased chances of showers through Sunday, all
remaining rather light across the region. Temperatures will cool
slightly over the weekend, with afternoon highs in the low to mid
50s, and morning lows staying steady in the upper 30s to lower
40s.

.LONG TERM /MONDAY THROUGH THURSDAY/...The unsettled pattern looks
to continue into the long term as the aforementioned upper level
trough shifts southward on Monday, keeping the chance of showers
in the forecast. Ensembles look to indicate a transient upper
level ridge moving over the region through midweek, but looks
rather weak. Therefore, kept chances of showers in the forecast
through the remainder of the long term. Temperatures will
generally stay in the low to mid 50s and overnight lows in the
upper 30s to lower 40s.
