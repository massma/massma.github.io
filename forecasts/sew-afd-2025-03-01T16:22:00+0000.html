.SHORT TERM /TODAY THROUGH MONDAY/...
-- Changed Discussion --
Satellite imagery shows
stratus over the offshore waters as well as areas of fog
developing in the river valleys. Areas of patchy fog will
continue to develop early this morning. This fog will once again
be quite shallow and should burn away by the late morning.

Clouds will increase today as a frontal system moves westward
towards the region. High temperatures will be notably cooler on
the coast where mostly cloudy conditions prevail all day--in the
low to mid 50s. Elsewhere, conditions will be fairly similar to
yesterday, with highs in the upper 50s to low 60s. Mid to upper
60s may be possible in the Cascade valleys.

Rain showers will arrive along the coast this afternoon, slowly
spreading inland overnight and continuing into Sunday and early
Monday. In the 36 hours ending Monday morning, total rainfall
amounts will be on on the order of one to two tenths of an inch
through most of the lowlands, with up to half an inch possible in
the mountains and along the Pacific coast. In an essence, Sunday
and Monday will be cool and drizzly. High temperatures will be
around 50 with low temperatures in the 40s.

.LONG TERM /TUESDAY THROUGH FRIDAY/...Increasing confidence that
Tuesday morning will be mostly dry as western Washington will lie
between two systems with a transient upper level ridge overhead.
The next trough will move through Tuesday night into Wednesday for
another round of light precipitation. Snow levels will lower to
around 3000 ft, allowing for light snow accumulations at the
passes during this time period. An upper level ridge looks to
build over the east Pacific Thursday into Friday with the trough
being pushed to the southeast. There`s a wide variety of potential
solutions to this setup depending on how prominent the ridge
becomes and how far east the trough goes. If the trough remains
closer towards California (with a higher amplitude ridge pushing
the jet into British Columbia), these are the drier solutions
which comprise about 45% of the total members. The remaining 55%
of solutions the trough is pushed towards the central U.S. with a
lower amplitude ridge and the jet stream aimed closer towards
Washington. This comprises the wetter solutions and may
potentially point towards the beginnings of a potential
atmospheric river setup. More to come as we get closer to next
weekend.
